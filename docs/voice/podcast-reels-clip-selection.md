# Podcast clip / pull-quote selection (Reels SOP)

_Verbatim copy of the "Instagram Reels SOP (Podcast Clips Only)" Google Doc. Written for
Reels, but the "what makes a good clip" criteria are exactly how to choose **pull quotes**
from an episode transcript for the article rebuilds._

**Objective:** Maximise reach, retention, and engagement on Instagram Reels using podcast video clips.

## The non-negotiable rule
The hook must land in the first 1–3 seconds. If the clip needs "context" to make sense, it's not the right clip.

## What makes a good clip / pull quote (choose ONE)
Prioritise moments that start with:
- A bold or emotionally charged statement
- A strong facial reaction or pause
- A vulnerable or honest admission
- A "hard truth" or unexpected insight
- A moment that feels mid-conversation (not staged)

Avoid: introductions; "so today we're talking about…"; slow build-ups; long explanations before the point.

## Clip length & structure (Reels)
Ideal 2–6 seconds, max 12. Quick cuts. Clips should loop naturally.

## Visual editing (Reels)
Full vertical 9:16. Speaker large on screen. Avoid wide shots that lose the expression. Subtitles on (clear, high contrast, minimal words).

## Text on screen (if used)
Reinforce the hook, don't repeat it. 7 words or fewer. Large, instantly readable. Examples: "This surprised me." / "No one talks about this." / "This changed everything."

## Audio & export
Clean, punchy audio (silence trimmed). Export optimised for Instagram.

## Editor mindset shift
Think less "Is this a good quote?" and more "Would someone stop scrolling for this *without context*?"

---

**Applied to article pull quotes:** pick the single strongest VERBATIM line per couple of
sections — a bold statement, a vulnerable admission, or a hard truth that lands without
setup. Never paraphrase or invent.
