02

Typography

EB Garamond does almost everything, just like your brand board. Headlines, intros, body, links. Sentence case or lowercase, one word in italic for lift. The caps face is Hanken Grotesk, spaced wide, standing in for Century Gothic from your logo.

Makeup, at its best, is like great lighting. It lifts, flatters and makes you feel instantly more alive without anyone guessing what's changed. This is the reading text of the site, serif and warm, the way your guides already read.

UI / navHanken Grotesk

Buttons, menus, captions and tags. The quiet sans that keeps the serif company without competing with it.

Times New Roman stays on social, for the newspaper-column posts. The Seasons stays your decorative social font. EB Garamond carries the website, so the two worlds rhyme without repeating.
03

Layout & space

White, open and hairline-ruled. The opposite of dense. One thing leads each screen, the rest support it, and the space between is left alone.

Hierarchy, not equality

One clear lead per screen, then smaller supporting cards. Never a flat grid of identical tiles.

Asymmetry on purpose

Large beside small, image stepping past a caption. Off-balance feels alive. Symmetry feels like a template.

Hairlines, not boxes

Thin rules and white space do the dividing, the way SheerLuxe does. No heavy cards, no shadows, no clutter.

Mobile is the original

Designed thumb-first, because that's where you arrive from. Desktop is the generous version of the phone.

04

Movement & image

Alive but calm

Soft scroll reveals, a slow zoom on hover, short muted video loops in heroes. Slow easings, nothing bouncy. If an animation shows off, it goes.

The gradient is the grade

Photography carries the colour. A black lower-third gradient sits under white text wherever a headline meets an image. Top third, bottom third, wherever the photo allows.

Product as still life

Shop imagery on white, styled as a flat-lay, not a product feed. The shop should look shot for the magazine.
