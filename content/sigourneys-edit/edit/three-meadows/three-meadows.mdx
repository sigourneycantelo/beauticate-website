[vc_row][vc_column][vc_single_image image="64505&#8243; img_size="full"][/vc_column][/vc_row][vc_row][vc_column][vc_column_text]I’m here with Damien and the kids on a content production trip for the B[eautiful Inside by Beauticate podcast](https://open.spotify.com/show/5su7l0yO5Ue0706K2Lzd8q?si=c05df2c654984ad1). There are so many great people to interview up in Byron and on the Gold Coast, so I wanted to plant myself somewhere central so that I could get around to all my locations easily. It was nice to bring the family on this trip with me so I could still spend some time with them, while also working my bottom off during the day. The kids were drawn by the promise of a pool and plenty of room to run around[/vc_column_text][vc_single_image image="64503&#8243; img_size="full"][/vc_column][/vc_row][vc_row][vc_column][vc_column_text]The moment we step inside, we’re struck by the sheer sense of calm. The house is airy and lofty, a dreamy palette of white and beige enriched with natural textures. Sunlight pours in through expansive windows, while raw timber beams—repurposed from the original orchard—add a grounding, rustic charm. The organic timber elements scattered throughout the house tell their own story, having been reclaimed from an old railway bridge. This clever balance of old and new runs through every detail of Three Meadows, giving it a warmth that feels so personal and considered.[/vc_column_text][/vc_column][/vc_row][vc_row][vc_column][vc_single_image image="64504&#8243; img_size="full"][/vc_column][/vc_row][vc_row][vc_column][vc_column_text]And it is personal. So much of what makes this place special comes from the vision of its owner, Aaron—a jeweller-turned-furniture-designer whose artistry is everywhere. From the handmade doors and light fittings to the stunning stone bathtubs, every corner of the house has been infused with thoughtfulness and craftsmanship. Even the objets d’art—delightful curios dotted throughout the home— lending a sense of connection to the space.[/vc_column_text][/vc_column][/vc_row][vc_row][vc_column][vc_single_image image="64511&#8243; img_size="full"][/vc_column][/vc_row][vc_row][vc_column][vc_column_text]I slip into one of the plush bathrobes (they are impossibly soft, by the way) and settle in to work on my notes for tomorrow’s interview with [Emma Lane – the creative behind The Range Estates](https://open.spotify.com/episode/0VuVsTCgRfFiZCGphYfeLp) who is at Copperstone, just around the corner in Bangalow. Damien sets himself up to work at the dining table and I head down to the office for some quiet. It’s the kind of house where you can spread out and have space while still feeling connected. There are plenty of private nooks for solitude, but also large, inviting living spaces to gather and reconnect.[/vc_column_text][/vc_column][/vc_row][vc_row][vc_column][vc_single_image image="64506&#8243; img_size="full"][/vc_column][/vc_row][vc_row][vc_column][vc_column_text]Outside, the grounds are just as captivating. Sculptures, crafted from repurposed oil drums, rise up like industrial flowers across the gardens. Some of these were even made in Aaron’s factory in Vietnam, adding a global nomadic charm to the estate’s landscape. The lawns stretch endlessly, and the gardens buzz with life, making you want to slow down, breathe deep, and truly take it all in.[/vc_column_text][/vc_column][/vc_row][vc_row][vc_column][vc_single_image image="64507&#8243; img_size="full"][/vc_column][/vc_row][vc_row][vc_column][vc_column_text]One of the things I love most about Three Meadows is how perfectly it balances relaxation and celebration. With multiple event spaces, it’s easy to see why this place is such a sought-after destination for weddings, retreats, or milestone celebrations. The spaces on offer?

- The Shed: A versatile, rustic-luxe space ideal for intimate ceremonies, recovery drinks, and long-table soirees. Its barn doors open wide to reveal breathtaking hinterland views, but it also works beautifully as a cozy, all-weather option.

- The Pavilion: A magical space crafted from reclaimed timber, complete with handmade chandeliers. It’s perfect for a long-table dinner under the stars, where you feel cocooned by nature with just the right amount of shelter.

- The Meadows: The expansive lawns and flowering gardens are made for elevated outdoor celebrations. Whether it’s a cocktail party or a wedding, it’s a backdrop that feels both intimate and spectacular, especially in the golden hour glow of Byron’s sunsets.

- The Cottage: Additional accommodation on site that sleeps four, the cottage is beautifully designed and features an outdoor stone bath as well as another epic bath and bathroom with a sliding door that opens fully to enjoy views of the garden while still feeling private.
