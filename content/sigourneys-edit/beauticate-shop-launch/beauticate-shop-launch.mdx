There's a question I get asked constantly. In DMs, in WhatsApp chats, at dinner parties, at school pickup. Sometimes from strangers, sometimes from women I've known for twenty years. The question is always some version of the same thing: *just tell me what to buy.*

They don't want a mood board or list of forty things they'll screenshot and never look at again. They want to know what I would actually choose. The LED mask I'd actually buy. The candle that's actually worth it. The fragrance that's been sitting on my bathroom shelf for three years, not because I forgot about it, but because I keep reaching for it.

I've been writing about beauty and wellness for twenty-six years. My first beauty writing gig was at an indie magazine called "Your Destiny" when I was 18 years old. From there I moved to TV Week where I had my own beauty and fashion page (it was there that I had to rub bronzing cream on Chris Hemsworth's chest during a cover shoot because the makeup artist had gone home. Someone had to do it!). Eventually I got a gig as Beauty Editor at New Woman magazine, then Glamour, then Vogue Australia, where I worked for the better part of a decade. Then, finally, Beauticate, which I founded in 2014 because I wanted to build something more personal than a magazine and more honest than the algorithm-driven beauty internet we were all starting to drown in. And for all of that time, the most useful thing I have apparently ever done is be the person my friends text when they need to know where to buy a good candle.

So we built a shop.

## The coffee that started it

It took a long time to get here, and the reason it took so long is that I kept hitting the same wall: logistics. I knew what the shop should be. I didn't know how to make it work without a warehouse, a fulfillment team, and a level of capital I didn't have. We launched our Top 100 Products list back in 2018, and I watched it drive thousands of clicks to external brand websites, losing my precious reader clicks in the process. Our Sacred Six edits on Instagram would sell products out. The Beautiful Inside podcast would convert listeners into customers for the founders we'd featured. But there was nowhere for any of that to land that was ours.

Then one day I was having coffee with Nicole O'Neil, the founder of Bonne Wellness. She asked if I would ever do a shop? I told her that it's too complicated to hold stock and warehouses etc and I'd thought about dropshipping but the quality can be questionable. And she said: have you looked at Modern Dropship?

I went home and looked it up, and I couldn't believe it. The technology had caught up with what I'd wanted to do for years. I could curate the products I wanted to feature and the brands would ship your order directly from their own warehouse. No stock. No fulfilment headaches. The editorial model I'd always wanted, finally technically possible. That was almost a year ago. The year that followed was not easy.

## What actually happened

I will tell the full story in my new Substack, The Hack Stack, where I'm planning to document the tech shortcuts, the AI tools, the productivity wins and the very expensive mistakes of building something like this more or less by yourself. But the short version is this: I hired a developer to build the shop. They built it. And then they locked me out of my own website because they had made it too technical for me to make changes myself. I had to start again.

What I discovered, rebuilding in Shopify myself, was that I didn't need a developer in the first place. Shopify is remarkable to use. It has a built-in AI called Sidekick that I've started referring to as Beauticate's MVP, because it has quietly saved me more times than I can count in the past few months. If you are thinking about building anything in e-commerce and you've been putting it off because you think it's too hard: it is less hard than you think. I am living proof.

## What the shop actually is

Beauticate Shop is not a marketplace. It's not a feed. It's an edit.

Every product on it has been tested, considered, chosen by me, by the Beauticate Collective, or by our guest curators. Nothing is there because a brand paid to be there. Everything is there because someone who has spent decades in this industry thinks it's worth your money.

The launch lineup brings together brands we've been quietly championing for years. Australian botanical skincare from [Mukti Organics](https://beauticate.shop/collections/mukti-organics). Fragrance from [Lumira](https://beauticate.shop/collections/lumira-1) and [Tulita](https://beauticate.shop/collections/tulita-parfum). Wellness from [JS Health](https://beauticate.shop/collections/jshealth-vitamins-aus) and [Eir Women](https://beauticate.shop/collections/eir-women). Skin from Mukti, SaintLouve, La Mav, and [Subtle Energies](https://beauticate.shop/collections/subtle-energies). One of my all-time favourite lifestyle brands, Maison Balzac. More brands are coming, including some that have already agreed and are just working through the onboarding process, which I will be honest, has been the hardest part of all of this.

If you've read Beauticate for a while, you'll recognise many of them. They've been on our pages, in our podcasts and in my own bathroom for years.

## What you'll find

The shop is built around two ways of browsing. You can shop by category when you know what you're looking for. Or you can shop by moment, which is how I actually shop: the [Deepest Sleep](https://beauticate.shop/collections/evening-unwind) edit when you are lying awake at midnight, the [Winter Edit](https://beauticate.shop/collections/autumn-edit) when you don't know where to start. That's what I've always wanted to build. A shop that thinks the way I think.

## How it works

Beauticate Shop works a little differently from most retailers. Rather than holding stock in a single warehouse, each brand we work with ships your order directly from theirs. It means your products come from the people who made them. It means the brands stay independent and we stay editorial. And it means that when you shop a curated edit, your order may arrive in several beautiful parcels, sometimes a few days apart.

We think it's worth it. The brands keep their craft. We keep our independence. And every order helps us keep making the editorial work that's brought you here.

## The Beauticate Collective

I'm not doing this alone.

The Beauticate Collective is a standing group of editors, facialists, doctors, nutritionists and beauty experts whose taste shapes the shop alongside mine. Kate Waterhouse on style. Jocelyn Petroni on skin. Rae Morris on makeup. Michelle Bridges on wellness. Camilla Thompson on nutrition. Plus more across every category we cover. You'll see their picks throughout the shop, and over time you'll hear from them directly through guest edits, columns and podcast appearances.

It's the most credible editorial board I've ever worked with, and I worked at Vogue.

## What I need from you

Beauticate Shop is launching in beta. We're proud of what we've built but we're still refining things, and we want to hear from you as we go. Tell us what works. Tell us what doesn't. Tell us what you wish was there. Your feedback shapes what we build next.

The shop will keep evolving over the months ahead. There are guest curator edits in the works, a launch event and many more exciting initiatives.

**Be one of the first**

There's something I want to offer the people who show up early. The ones who have been here for years, and the ones finding us for the first time this week.

We're building a membership. Not a points scheme you forget you joined. Something closer to an inner circle. Early access to new edits and drops. First invitations to events. A way of being part of the shop rather than just shopping it. It opens properly later this year, and I'm building it slowly and carefully, the way I've built everything else.

But founding members aren't decided by a sign-up. They're the women who shop with us first. When the full program opens, they'll already be in, at the top, with the best of everything we've planned. You can't buy your way to that later. You can only be early.

So if you've been waiting for somewhere like this, this is the moment. Shop the launch, and you're one of us from the start.
