### **Take a quick shower (ideally under 4 minutes).**

You can do it! Since we use up to 45 liters of water per minute while lathering up, reducing your minutes in the shower can make a big difference. You can also replace your showerhead to reduce the liters you use while washing (get a water-saving showerhead here). One of my favourite ways to limit shower time is by playing a pump-up song in the bathroom. Not only does it put me in a great mood to start the day, when the song ends I know that I should start to rinse off and finish up![/vc_column_text][/vc_column_inner][/vc_row_inner][vc_row_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_column_text]

### **Brush your teeth and wash your face in the sink**.

And make sure you turn off the faucet while you brush and scrub![/vc_column_text][/vc_column_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_single_image image="21732&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][/vc_column_inner][/vc_row_inner][vc_row_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_single_image image="21733&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][/vc_column_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_column_text]

### **Indulged in a nice bath? Consider using the water to spritz house plants or your lawn.**

Showers typically use much less water than the average bath, but when you need to soak at the end of a long day reusing the water from your tub is an awesome way to conserve H2O.[/vc_column_text][/vc_column_inner][/vc_row_inner][vc_row_inner][vc_column_inner][vc_custom_heading text="OK WHAT ABOUT THE KITCHEN&#8230;" font_container="tag:h2|font_size:20px|text_align:left"][/vc_column_inner][/vc_row_inner][vc_row_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_single_image image="21734&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][vc_column_text]PHOTO BY @IKEA[/vc_column_text][/vc_column_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

### Keep a reusable water bottle in the fridge overnight.

This way you don’t have to run

the tap water until it cools down when you fill up your glass in the morning![/vc_column_text][/vc_column_inner][/vc_row_inner][vc_row_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

### **Don’t run the dishwasher unless it’s completely full.**

And if you don’t have a dishwasher? Plug the sink and wash your dishes in a soapy bath instead of keeping the faucet on, then use a basin of water to rinse them off.[/vc_column_text][vc_column_text]

### **Don’t get rid of water that you used to boil foods!**

Use pasta or veggie water to hydrate your plants instead.[/vc_column_text][/vc_column_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_single_image image="21741&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][vc_column_text]PHOTO BY @SHOPLUXCLOTHING[/vc_column_text][/vc_column_inner][/vc_row_inner][vc_row_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_single_image image="21743&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][vc_column_text]PHOTO BY @KARLYKLOSS[/vc_column_text][/vc_column_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

### **Make Mondays meatless.**

The water used to produce a piece of meat is much higher than what it takes to make corresponding servings of things like beans, veggies, and grains. Less than a single kilogram of beef consumes 6800 liters of water while the same amount of wheat  consumes 522 [litres](http://www.gracelinks.org/blog/1143/beef-the-king-of-the-big-water-footprints). Cutting down on your meat consumption can significantly lower your personal contribution to shrinking water reserves.[/vc_column_text][/vc_column_inner][/vc_row_inner][vc_row_inner][vc_column_inner][vc_custom_heading text="OTHER HANDY TIPS…" font_container="tag:h2|font_size:20px|text_align:left" css_animation="fadeIn"][/vc_column_inner][/vc_row_inner][vc_row_inner][vc_column_inner][vc_column_text]

### **Wash your clothes in cold water.**

Not only will it keep them from shrinking and fading, it will reduce the amount of electricity your washing machine uses (90% of it goes into heating water).[/vc_column_text][/vc_column_inner][/vc_row_inner][vc_row_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_single_image image="21758&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][vc_column_text]

We love these. And don't water your lawn every day, most grasses will thrive with a watering schedule of every 5-10 days.[/vc_column_text][/vc_column_inner][/vc_row_inner][vc_row_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]
