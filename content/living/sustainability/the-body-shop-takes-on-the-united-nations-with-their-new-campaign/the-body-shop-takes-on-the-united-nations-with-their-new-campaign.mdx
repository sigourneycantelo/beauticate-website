The Body Shop, no stranger to dedicating themselves to a good cause, have decided to take on lobbying the United Nations for an international ban on animal testing for all beauty products and brands.

Their new campaign, Forever Against Animal Testing, in collaboration with non-profit Cruelty Free International, aims to completely ban animal testing on an international scale. To do so, they will need 8 million signatures to sign their petition in order to take their case to the UN by August 2018.

While this is a new campaign, it definitely isn’t the first time that these two companies have tackled animal testing. In fact, The Body Shop was the first global beauty brand to campaign for cruelty free cosmetics in 1989. They continued their fight throughout the years and eventually joined forces with Cruelty Free International. Together, they were able to reach the one million signatures needed to implement the EU ban in 2013, inspiring other countries such as South Korea, India, New Zealand and Australia (which will implement its ban starting July of this year) to do the same.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What is The Body Shop's Forever Against Animal Testing campaign?**
A: Forever Against Animal Testing is a campaign launched by The Body Shop in collaboration with non-profit Cruelty Free International, aiming to secure an international ban on animal testing for all beauty products at the United Nations. They needed 8 million petition signatures to take the case to the UN by August 2018.

**Q: Was The Body Shop the first brand to campaign against animal testing?**
A: Yes. The Body Shop was the first global beauty brand to campaign for cruelty-free cosmetics, starting back in 1989. Working with Cruelty Free International, they helped achieve the EU ban on animal testing in cosmetics in 2013, inspiring bans in South Korea, India, New Zealand and Australia soon after.

**Q: Which countries have banned animal testing in cosmetics?**
A: The EU implemented its ban in 2013 after The Body Shop and Cruelty Free International gathered one million petition signatures. South Korea, India, New Zealand and Australia have since followed, with Australia's ban beginning in July 2017.
