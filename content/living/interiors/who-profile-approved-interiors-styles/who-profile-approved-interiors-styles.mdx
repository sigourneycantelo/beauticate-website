## MINIMALIST STYLE WITH [HAYLEY BONHAM](https://www.beauticate.com/who/hayley-bonham-stylist-publicist-entrepreneur?rq=Bonham)

[/vc_column_text][vc_single_image image="30805&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][vc_row_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_single_image image="30806&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][/vc_column_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_single_image image="30807&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][/vc_column_inner][/vc_row_inner][vc_single_image image="30808&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

### 1.     Invest in quality furniture

Your biggest pieces of furniture are what set the tone for the room – so don’t be afraid to spend on staple items that will form the backbone for the space – like Hayley’s ultra-chic [Keilhauer chairs](https://www.keilhauer.com/products/vanilla.html) in her office at La Porte Space.[/vc_column_text][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

### 2.     Pare things back

It’s cliché, but minimalistic style truly calls for quality over quantity. Think a few choice coffee table books (not dozens), a set of matching candles, and an assortment of well-selected trinkets – knowing when to stop is vital.[/vc_column_text][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

### 3.   Comfort is key

Minimalism need not necessarily make for a cold and clinical space. Cosy but curated features like fireplaces or soft rugs (all in a seamlessly tonal colour palette) add warmth, making you feel right at home.[/vc_column_text][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

## ECLECTIC À LA [LUCY FOLK](https://www.beauticate.com/who/lucy-folk-jeweller-designer?rq=lucy%20folk)

[/vc_column_text][vc_single_image image="30809&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][vc_row_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_single_image image="30810&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][/vc_column_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_single_image image="30811&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][/vc_column_inner][/vc_row_inner][vc_single_image image="30812&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

### 1. Add pops of colour, fabric and textures

The key to an eclectic style is decorating with unique accents that simultaneously clash and complement one another. The mustard linen fabric of Lucy’s sofa beautifully contrasts the glistening texture of the bright blue pool pictures, but the primary colours also pair together seamlessly.[/vc_column_text][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

### 2. Fabrics should be chosen for comfort

Fabrics elicit different feelings and moods – and luckily an eclectic style allows for flexibility to create distinct tones within a space. We recommend sticking to fabrics that make you feel good and are simple (such as Lucy’s linen duvet), so that smaller accents in the room can become the focal points.[/vc_column_text][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

### 3. Keep your walls white and your accents colourful

White walls are a good foundation for an eclectic look, allowing maximum use of colour in your décor. A boldly-coloured wall in an eclectically styled home can make the space feel cluttered and unorganised, whereas white walls keep things looking clean.[/vc_column_text][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

## GLAMOROUS LIKE [MICHELLE BRIDGES](https://www.beauticate.com/who/michelle-bridges-personal-trainer-author?rq=michel)

[/vc_column_text][vc_single_image image="30813&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][vc_row_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_single_image image="30814&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][/vc_column_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_single_image image="30815&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][/vc_column_inner][/vc_row_inner][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

### 1.     Go for black and white, with clever use of colour

Michelle’s monochromatic dining room looks crisp and clean against her Sydney skyline view. The pop of pink flower assortments throughout her home adds visual interest and makes the space feel warmer, while remaining elegant and pared back.[/vc_column_text][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

### 2.     Introduce metallic Accents

Gold and silver décor make a Hollywood Regency home. Metal scatters light extremely well, so it’s ideal for brightening up a room (perfect if your space doesn’t receive a lot of natural light). Michelle masterfully uses touches of silver, gold and rose gold accent pieces throughout her space to add a luxurious flair.[/vc_column_text][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

## BEACH CHIC, [DARREN PALMER & OLIVIER DUVILLARD](https://www.beauticate.com/who/darren-palmer-interiors-designer-and-olivier-duvillard?rq=Darren) STYLE

[/vc_column_text][vc_single_image image="30816&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][vc_row_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_single_image image="30817&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][/vc_column_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_single_image image="30818&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][/vc_column_inner][/vc_row_inner][vc_single_image image="30819&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

### 1.    Stick to blue and beige

It should come as no surprise that a beach chic home should make your guests feel like they’re – you guessed it – really at the beach. Blue and beige are natural coastal tones, so opt for pieces in these shades to set this tone.[/vc_column_text][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

### 2.    Incorporate marine décor

When it comes to beach chic style, it’s definitely all in the details. Darren and Olivier have decorated their shelves with beachside pieces, like corals and shells in different shapes and sizes, to evoke a coastal setting.[/vc_column_text][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

### 3.     Add some earthy tones with natural textures

Woven wicker, raffia and unfinished wood in neutral tones add character to a space without introducing overwhelming colours. Artful touches of these materials bring a relaxed, laidback feel throughout Darren and Olivier’s home,[/vc_column_text][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

## BOHEMIAN LIKE [JUJU ROCHE](https://www.beauticate.com/who/juju-roche-artist?rq=juju)

[/vc_column_text][vc_single_image image="30820&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][vc_row_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_single_image image="30821&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][/vc_column_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_single_image image="30822&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][/vc_column_inner][/vc_row_inner][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

### 1.     Use muted colours

Dusty roses, maroons, and faded blues – these all create a rustic feeling that ties into the bohemian aesthetic.[/vc_column_text][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

### 2.     Incorporate different shades of wood

Similar to an eclectic style, bohemian homes mix and match textures and tones. Juju’s full length mirror, table, chair and lamp are all made of contrasting wood finishes, yet pair together beautifully.[/vc_column_text][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]
