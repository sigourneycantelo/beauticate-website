### I find the busier I am, the harder my wardrobe has to work. It’s definitely worth looking for multi-tasking essentials that won’t leave you staring at your closet in the morning, scratching your head. Since I work from a home office, I’ve found that on non-meeting days, my wardrobe has naturally become more comfortable and my weekend-wear often morphs into day-wear. For functions I might reach for a dress and heels, but I’ve found myself investing in great denim pieces worked back with classic white and grey for most days. So here’s our list of essentials and a range of steal to splurge shop the look options.

## 5. THE TAILORED BLAZER

[/vc_column_text][/vc_column_inner][/vc_row_inner][vc_row_inner][vc_column_inner][vc_single_image image="29434&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

SIGOURNEY WEARS TOMMY BLAZER (ON SALE NOW AT DAVID JONES); AND TOMMY SHIRT (ALSO ON SALE) AND MIH JEANS.

[/vc_column_text][/vc_column_inner][/vc_row_inner][vc_row_inner][vc_column_inner][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

I love preppy style – always have.  It just looks pulled together (when you feel anything but!) and a blazer is a chic way to dress up jeans to a go-anywhere level. (Country Club, here we come.) For a well-cut style look to those all-American brands like Tommy, Ralph Lauren, Gant and J Crew. Having said that, I bought a navy Gucci blazer at the Woodbury Common Outlets in New York about ten years ago and I still wear it constantly. Navy is the perfect blazer shade. It offsets white beautifully, it pairs with denim and it just feels perfectly pitched no matter what the weather.

## 6. THE STATEMENT TOP

[/vc_column_text][/vc_column_inner][/vc_row_inner][vc_row_inner][vc_column_inner][vc_single_image image="28475&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

[/vc_column_text][/vc_column_inner][/vc_row_inner][vc_row_inner][vc_column_inner][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

Sometimes plain tees and jumpers just feel a bit…well… boring. While I’ve never really been a big logo girl I’ve found that a statement top or tee is a cute way to add a bit of personality to your outfit. To stop it from looking like too teeny-bopper-chainstore ensure you invest in a quality fabric – cashmere, wool or soft cotton – and cut. Kenzo, Bella Freud, [Zoe Karssen](http://bit.ly/1NCfX8O), and Markus Lupfer do some awesome slogan jumpers and tees.
