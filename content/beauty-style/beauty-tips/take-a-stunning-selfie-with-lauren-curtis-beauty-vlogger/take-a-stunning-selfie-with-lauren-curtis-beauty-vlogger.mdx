  - question: "What lighting is best for taking flattering selfies?"
    answer: "Natural light is your best friend when it comes to selfies, as it minimises harsh shadows and reveals your true skin tone. Position yourself near a window or outdoors during golden hour for the most flattering results. Avoid direct overhead lighting, which can create unflattering shadows under the eyes and cheekbones."
  - question: "How do you find your best selfie angle?"
    answer: "Everyone's face is different, so finding your angle requires a bit of experimentation. Generally, holding the camera slightly above eye level and tilting your chin down slightly tends to be flattering for most people. Take multiple shots from different angles to discover what works best for your face shape."
  - question: "Should you use filters for selfies?"
    answer: "While filters can enhance a selfie, the most stunning results often come from good lighting, angle, and skincare rather than heavy editing. If you do use filters, opt for subtle ones that enhance rather than completely transform your appearance. The goal is to look like the best version of yourself, not unrecognisable."
  - question: "Does makeup affect how you look in selfies?"
    answer: "Yes, makeup can make a significant difference in how you appear in photos. Focus on well-blended foundation, defined brows, and a touch of colour on the cheeks and lips to add dimension. Matte or satin finishes photograph better than overly shiny products, which can create unwanted glare on camera."
