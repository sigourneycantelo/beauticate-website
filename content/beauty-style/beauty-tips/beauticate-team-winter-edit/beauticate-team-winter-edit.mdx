## The Pieces We’re Reaching for Right Now

<PortraitQuote src="/content/beauty-style/beauty-tips/beauticate-team-winter-edit/sig-scaled.png" alt="Sigourney Cantelo in a structured blazer holding Goldfield and Banks fragrance for the Beauticate winter edit" name="Sigourney Cantelo" role="Publisher, Beauticate" side="left">

"Winter always makes me gravitate towards pieces that feel a little more structured and tactile. And I love the sharp, sculptural feel of a great blazer. It instantly pulls everything together. So, it becomes an easy foundation for everything else. Also, a small flash of gold, like a softly scalloped hoop earring, catches the light through hair and adds that subtle polish. Fragrance-wise, I’ve been wearing [**Goldfield & Banks**](https://t.cfjump.com/68150/t/90704?Url=https%3a%2f%2fwww.goldfieldandbanks.com%2fproducts%2fpurple-suede) Purple Suede constantly. It’s smoky, woody, a little bit sexy. The kind of**[scent](https://www.beauticate.com/beauty-style/beauty-tips/6-gorgeous-scents-to-upgrade-your-autumn-fragrance-wardrobe/)** that lingers on knits and scarves. When my skin inevitably starts to feel dry or slightly irritated, [**Mukti**](https://muktiorganics.com/) Marigold Cream is deeply nourishing and the scent makes it feel like a proper**[ritual](https://www.beauticate.com/sigourneys-edit/winter-beauty-rituals/)**. Also, I've been leaning into texture more this season. A woven leather shoe, something slightly unexpected, can add an extra element of interest."

</PortraitQuote>

“Chocolate brown is having a moment and I’m embracing it. It feels softer than black but just as polished, and works beautifully layered tone-on-tone. I’ve been living in cosy, elevated staples that instantly make you feel put-together. A great coat is non-negotiable for winter. It’s the hero piece you build every outfit around. I love timeless, flattering styles that are easy to throw over anything and instantly elevate even the simplest look. A white or cream base layer is the foundation of so many outfits, whether worn on its own or layered under knits and coats. Look for pieces that feel warm without bulk and pair effortlessly with everything. I’ve also been leaning into richer, earthy tones at home. Think terracotta, sienna and warm clay hues that instantly make a space feel more inviting as the weather cools. It’s such an easy way to transition interiors into winter with minimal effort but maximum impact. As the temperature drops, I also switch to richer, more nourishing skincare. It’s all about skin feeling comforted and radiant by morning.”

</PortraitQuote>

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What fragrances does the Beauticate team recommend for winter?**
A: Sigourney recommends Goldfield & Banks Purple Suede for its smoky, woody warmth that lingers on knits and scarves. Rae Morris and Michelle Bridges both wear Goldfield & Banks Silky Woods. Dr Leanne Girgis wears Tulita Agati. Dr Amy Chahal reaches for Lumira Cuban Tobacco. All lean into richer, earthy tones that suit cooler months.

**Q: What skincare changes should you make in winter?**
A: The Beauticate team recommends switching to richer, more nourishing formulations. Key picks include Mukti Marigold Hydrating Cream for deep nourishment, Rationale No.3 Tinted Serum SPF50 for sheer coverage with sun protection, Sodashi Enzyme Face Polish for gentle exfoliation, and Elemis Pro-Collagen Cleansing Balm for a thorough evening cleanse that doesn’t strip.

**Q: What wellness habits does the Beauticate team prioritise in winter?**
A: Camilla Thompson focuses on red light therapy post-sauna for skin and energy, and adds creatine to her morning routine for focus. Jacqueline Allwill uses an infrared sauna for recovery and circulation. Michelle Bridges maintains consistent home workouts and wears a WHOOP tracker to keep sleep and recovery honest.

**Q: What are the best winter fashion picks from the Beauticate team?**
