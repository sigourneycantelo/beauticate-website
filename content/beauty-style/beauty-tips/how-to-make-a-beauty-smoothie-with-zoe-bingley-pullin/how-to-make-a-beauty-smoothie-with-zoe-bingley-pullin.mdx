## RECIPE

### Ingredients

**Protein Mix**

- 10 organic dried figs (diced, hard stalk removed)

- Organic dried dates

- ½ cup of brazil nuts

- ½ Pepitas (sunflower seeds)

- ½ cup of macadamias

- 3 tbsp honey

- 1/3 cup cocoa or carob powder

- 1 cup of walnuts

- 4 – 5 cocao beans

**Balls**

- 1 cup LSA powder (linseed, sunflower seeds and almonds)

- ½ cup fresh organic raspberries

### Recipe

- Add all the protein mix ingredients except for the Carob Powder to a blender and blend on high.

- Add the carob powder separately a bit at a time until the mix takes on a doughy consistency.

![Berry smoothie p 4](/content/beauty-style/beauty-tips/how-to-make-a-beauty-smoothie-with-zoe-bingley-pullin/Berry-Smoothie-P-4-1.jpg)


- Remove mixture from the blender. Take a small handful of the protein mix (about half a golf ball size) and make a boat shape with your hands.

- Insert a raspberry into the centre and then generously cover with more protein mixture to create the other half.

- Roll it the mixture in your hands to form a ball to roughly a golf ball size.

- Roll the balls into a coating of LSA powder to cover and they're ready to enjoy.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What is LSA powder and where can I buy it?**
A: LSA is a blend of ground linseed, sunflower seeds and almonds. It is available at most health food stores and many supermarkets.

**Q: Why is the carob or cocoa powder added separately?**
A: Adding it gradually allows you to control the consistency of the mixture. You stop when it reaches a doughy texture that can be shaped and rolled.

**Q: Can I swap out the figs or dates for other dried fruit?**
A: Yes, other soft dried fruits such as apricots or prunes will bind the mixture in a similar way, though they will change the flavour slightly.
