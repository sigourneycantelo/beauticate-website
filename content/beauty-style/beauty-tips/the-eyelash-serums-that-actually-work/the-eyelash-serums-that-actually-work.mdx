## THE LASH SERUM CONTROVERSY

A class of drugs called prostaglandin analogues were originally used by doctors to treat glaucoma patients, but a cosmetic side effect was noticed: patients started growing longer, denser and darker lashes. Companies capitalised on this accidental discovery and began using this class of drugs as the active ingredient in lash amplifying serums. Indeed, my former ultimate lash-boosting serum –[ Lilash ](https://www.lilash.com/products/lilash?gclid=Cj0KCQiAvqDiBRDAARIsADWh5Tdv18ckBqxohbU707z3HWERt-EQjsCJQheh7F7He7LZj37yY-V6U5YaAhcvEALw_wcB)– contains a form of this drug. But in May 2018, the Department of Health banned [Lilash](https://www.lilash.com/products/lilash?gclid=Cj0KCQiAvqDiBRDAARIsADWh5Tdv18ckBqxohbU707z3HWERt-EQjsCJQheh7F7He7LZj37yY-V6U5YaAhcvEALw_wcB). Why? According to the Therapeutic Goods Administration, prostaglandin analogue is a Schedule Four substance – meaning, legally, it can only be obtained via prescription. (It’s reportedly still available online and the company apparently ships worldwide.)

Latisse is the only legal lash product available on the Australian market that contains prostaglandin analogue, according to The Manse Clinic. Available by prescription only, Latisse is the primary treatment for hypotrichosis – a medical condition where eyelashes can't grow. “Latisse not only helped fill in the gaps and fix damaged eyelashes, but my lashes are much thicker overall,” Dr. Catherine Begovic, American Board Certified Plastic surgeon, reported. For all its lash boosting accolades, there is a reason it is prescription only: prostaglandin analogues can induce inflammation, which can lead to irritation. It also increases melanin production, so there’s a possibility dark pigments may form in lighter eyes, and surrounding skin can become discoloured. So, consult a doctor first and obtain a legal prescription – don’t fall prey to cheap online deals.

## BEST DIY: GREEN TEA

### The rave:

Beauty pageant veteran Patricia Ella Evangelista relied on a pantry staple to look fresh and stage-ready, even without a stitch of eye makeup. She placed green tea bags over her eyes, to tighten the area and reduce under-eye puffiness. And she noticed a bonus effect: her lashes became thicker and darker, and sparse areas began rapidly growing fine baby hairs. “I knew it wasn't a placebo effect, because when I stopped applying green tea, I noticed my lashes reverted back to their thinning ways,” Patricia explains. So brew some green tea (wait for it to cool down, of course) then use a q-tip to swipe the tea along your lashline. You’re now well on your way to luscious lashes.

### Key Ingredients:

Green tea contains EGCG, an antioxidant that has been clinically proven to promote hair growth and activate lazy hair cells. Meanwhile, vitamin B5 strengthens hair follicles and softens hair texture. Green tea also inhibits DHT - a hormone that destroys hair follicles and causes baldness. So get steeping.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What is the best eyelash serum that actually works?**
A: The article recommends EyEnvy as the best all-rounder, with multiple women crediting it for noticeably thicker, longer and darker lashes. Shu Uemura Lash Builder is the top pick for conditioning and repairing damaged lashes, while Grande Cosmetics LASH-MD is best for length, with results visible after two months of use.

**Q: Why was Lilash banned in Australia?**
A: Lilash contains a prostaglandin analogue, which was classified as a Schedule Four substance by the Therapeutic Goods Administration, meaning it can only be legally obtained via prescription. It was banned in Australia in May 2018. Latisse is the only prescription lash product legally available in Australia that contains this ingredient.

**Q: Can green tea help grow eyelashes?**
A: Yes, according to the article. Green tea contains EGCG, an antioxidant clinically proven to promote hair growth and activate dormant hair follicles. It also contains vitamin B5 to strengthen follicles and inhibits DHT, a hormone that destroys hair follicles. Apply cooled green tea along the lash line with a cotton bud for best results.

**Q: Are prostaglandin lash serums safe?**
A: They carry risks. Prostaglandin analogues can induce inflammation and irritation, increase melanin production which may darken lighter eyes, and cause discolouration of surrounding skin. Consulting a doctor and obtaining a legal prescription before use is strongly advised.
