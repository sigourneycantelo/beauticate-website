### Pop on a pillow

There are few things more soothing than snuggling up on the sofa, hot drink in tow, on a wintry evening – so why not up the cosy factor with some blissfully soft pillows? We love these Oly Mohair Scatter Cushions from Coco Republic – stuffed with downy soft feathers, they are a beautiful (and comfortable) addition to your winter décor. Plus, they come in a range of wintry hues – think russet, forest green and rich purple.

### Bring nature in

You may not be used to bringing gorgeous bunches of flowers into your home in the cooler months, but there is something to be said for the beauty of a wintry bouquet – we love winter roses, sweet peas and lavender, which all flower during the winter. Pair your seasonable blooms with this vase from Studio Twocan – inspired by the earthen tones of the Australian landscape, it would also be the perfect complement to a beautiful native floral arrangement.

### Cosy crockery

Elevate your cold-weather comfort food with some seasonally-inspired kitchenware. These gorgeous Temple & Webster bowls, in tones of grey, cream and charcoal, would pair perfectly with oh-so-delicious wintry fares, from cosy soups to hearty stews.

### Soften up

An easy way to nail winter décor is to incorporate soft textures, such as wools, silks and furs – they are comforting to touch, with the handy bonus of keeping you nice and warm. If you find it hard to pull yourself out of your toasty bed on frosty mornings, why not pop this cosy IKEA sheepskin next to your bed – it’s much kinder than floorboards to chilly feet when you haven’t yet had the chance to put on your slippers. And the stormy grey hue is ultra chic, too.

### Diffuse some warmth

Adding a warming, enveloping scent is a no-brainer trick to making a house instantly more welcoming. Our pick? The Charcoal diffuser from West Elm, with its wintry notes of smoke and heady sandalwood – it’s the perfect fragrance to make any space more inviting.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What are the easiest ways to make your home feel cosier in winter?**
A: The article recommends five simple steps: adding soft mohair or feather-stuffed cushions, bringing in seasonal winter blooms, upgrading your crockery with cosy stoneware, incorporating soft textures like wool and sheepskin, and diffusing a warm, enveloping scent such as sandalwood.

**Q: What flowers bloom in winter in Australia?**
A: Winter roses, sweet peas and lavender all flower during the cooler months, making them perfect choices for bringing a natural, seasonal touch to your interiors without needing to wait for spring.

**Q: What scent is recommended for making a home feel instantly welcoming in winter?**
A: The article picks the West Elm Charcoal diffuser with its notes of smoke and heady sandalwood as the perfect fragrance to make any space more inviting during the winter months.
