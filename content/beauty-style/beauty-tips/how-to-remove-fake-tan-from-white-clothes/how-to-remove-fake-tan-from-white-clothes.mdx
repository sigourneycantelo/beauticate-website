faqs:
  - question: "How do you get fake tan stains out of white sheets?"
    answer: "Act quickly by flipping the sheets and running cold water over the underside until it runs clear. Apply dishwashing soap to a clean rag and gently rub the affected area, then wash on a warm cycle with a white-specific stain remover. For stubborn stains on white fabrics, bleach can be used as a final treatment option."
  - question: "What's the best way to remove foundation stains from clothing?"
    answer: "Dish soap is ideal for foundation stains since it cuts through oil. Rub it lightly across the fabric and leave for about 20 minutes before washing. For oil-free foundations, shaving foam works brilliantly—apply it to the stain, wait a few minutes, then rinse in cold water and repeat as needed."
  - question: "Can you use bleach on fake tan stains in white clothes?"
    answer: "Yes, bleach can effectively remove fake tan stains from white fabrics like sheets. However, always check garment care tags first before using bleach on clothing, as some white garments may not be bleach-safe or could be damaged by it."
  - question: "Why should you flip clothes before rinsing beauty stains?"
    answer: "Flipping the fabric and rinsing from the underside prevents pushing the stain deeper into the fibres. This technique is particularly effective for fake tan and makeup stains, as it allows the product to rinse away from the fabric rather than through it."

	mso-ansi-language:EN-US;}Firstly, if you can, act fast, no matter how less-than-ideal you’re feeling from last night’s champagne. How to remove fake tan from your white clothes or sheets? Flip the clothing or sheets and run the underside (not the part that made contact with the tan) under cold water until it runs clear. Next, put dishwashing soap onto a clean rag and rub it lightly over the affected area. Then wash the sheets or fabric on a warm cycle with an added white specific stain remover. Repeat if necessary. If this doesn’t work (if the fabric is white) you can treat the discoloured area with bleach. This should be fine with your sheets, but check garment tags first.
