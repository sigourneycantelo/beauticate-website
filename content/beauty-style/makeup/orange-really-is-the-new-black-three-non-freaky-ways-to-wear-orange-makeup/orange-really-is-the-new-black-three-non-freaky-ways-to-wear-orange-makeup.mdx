The new generation of lip and cheek textures are sheer and buildable, so you’re not going to look like a clown. They provide just a wash of colour that, when paired with a generous brush of bronzer (Nars, I’m talking to you), give you a gorgeous, burnished Just-Back-From-The-Beach glow that is way more modern and fun than your standard makeup fare. Here are three easy ways to wear it, that won’t make you feel in any way freaky.

-

## A very chic lipstick

With their magnetic click-top lids and sleek colour-blocked packaging, Hermès lipstick is unashamedly luxe. It even comes packaged in a little canvas bag and the trademark orange box with brown ribbon.  What could be more Hermès, then, than the Hermès Orange Lipstick in Orange Boîte? Yes it’s bold, but you can also sheer it down with a wash of gloss. The more I wear it, the more I love it and it looks so good with a bit of self tan/bronzer (this limited edition [Nars Paradise Found](https://www.mecca.com.au/nars/paradise-found-bronzing-powder/I-043697.html) one is pretty spectacular). At $94, it is an investment, so a budget friendly option is L’Oréal Colour Riche Made For Me in Orange Fever.

-

## Just a wash of lip oil

The truly colour-shy can dip their toe in the trend with a bright lip oil such as Dior Lip Glow Oil in Coral. Blended with cherry oil, it’s so hydrating for the lips and subtly adapts to each wearer’s lip colour for a ‘your-lips-but-better’ hue. It’s also a joy to use. Like a lip balm and oil hybrid, it glides on like a dream but stays on like a champ. It’s definitely summer’s take on lip balm and a more modern formulation to old school lip gloss.  Another affordable option is Revlon Kiss Glow Lip Oil in M.V.Peach.

## Tangerine Cheek Stain

Again – this one’s a lot less scary than it looks in the pot. Being a stain, [Lilah B Divine Duo Lip & Cheek in b daring](https://www.mecca.com.au/lilah-b/divine-duo-lip-cheek-b-daring/I-037568.html) is buildable. Just dot it on the apples of the cheeks with your fingers and blend, baby, blend. Again, I love it teamed with a beautiful warm base and dash of bronzer, but it’s also really pretty on creamy skintones. You can also use a dab of it on your lips as a two-in-one and it unifies the face beautifully. A well-priced dupe of this one is Luma Just A Touch Lip & Cheek Tint in Signorita.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How do you wear orange makeup without it looking too bold or clownish?**
A: The key is choosing sheer, buildable formulas rather than heavy pigments. A sheer lip oil like Dior Lip Glow Oil in Coral, a buildable cheek stain, or a bold lipstick sheered down with gloss all allow you to control the intensity. Pairing any orange tone with bronzer helps create a cohesive, sun-kissed result rather than a jarring statement.

**Q: What are the best orange makeup products to try?**
A: The article recommends three options at different commitment levels: the Hermes Orange Boite lipstick for a luxe, bold choice; Dior Lip Glow Oil in Coral for a subtle wash of colour; and Lilah B Divine Duo Lip and Cheek in B. Daring as a buildable, two-in-one option. Budget alternatives include L'Oreal Colour Riche in Orange Fever and Revlon Kiss Glow Lip Oil in M.V. Peach.

**Q: Can you use an orange cheek stain on both lips and cheeks?**
A: Yes - the Lilah B Divine Duo Lip and Cheek in B. Daring is designed for both. Dot it on the apples of the cheeks, blend with fingers, and a small amount can also be used on the lips for a unified, tonal look that works especially well with a warm base and bronzer.
