STEP 1: BLOWDRY WITH VOLUME

[/vc_column_text][/vc_column_inner][/vc_row_inner][vc_row_inner][vc_column_inner][vc_single_image image="28487&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][/vc_column_inner][/vc_row_inner][vc_row_inner][vc_column_inner][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]“Smooth the natural texture out of the hair to just get a nice, clean consistency,” says Brad. “I’ve just used a little bit of root volume spray, a mousse, for a little bit of grip into the hair and then we’re just smoothing it out. The trick to a smooth shiny blowdry is to direct the nozzle of the hair dryer down the shaft of the hair with a big round barrel brush.”[/vc_column_text][/vc_column_inner][/vc_row_inner][vc_row_inner][vc_column_inner][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

STEP 2: CREATE WAVES WITH AN IRON

[/vc_column_text][/vc_column_inner][/vc_row_inner][vc_row_inner][vc_column_inner][vc_single_image image="28487&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][/vc_column_inner][/vc_row_inner][vc_row_inner][vc_column_inner][vc_column_text]“Use either a straightening iron like I did here, twisting the hair back on itself,” says Brad. “or I've recently discovered Ego Professional Twist – a new tool to the market that has a series of bobbles along the length of the wand creating waves but even heat distribution.

Take sections about 4 cm x 4cm and from the mid length wrap the hair away from the face run the iron or tong up and down the hair like a bungee jump this gives hair "movement" be sure over curl the ends , its OK if they are left straight.

Do this pattern around the head till all the hair is styled. I like L'Oreal Professional plus a nifty setting lotion that locks in the movement while protecting from heat.

Important to let the hair cool down completely this gives you a longer lasting curl/movement

[/vc_column_text][/vc_column_inner][/vc_row_inner][vc_row_inner][vc_column_inner][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

STEP 3: CREATE GRIT AND TEXTURE WITH HAIR POWDER

[/vc_column_text][/vc_column_inner][/vc_row_inner][vc_row_inner][vc_column_inner][vc_single_image image="28490&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][/vc_column_inner][/vc_row_inner][vc_row_inner][vc_column_inner][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]“Sprinkle some [L'Oreal Professionnel Texture Dust](http://lorealprofessionnel.co.uk/products/styling/tecniart/dusts/texturedust) within the hair this will give you the Beach texture,” says Brad.” Use your fingers to massage it into the roots, creating volume and ‘oomph’.[/vc_column_text][/vc_column_inner][/vc_row_inner][vc_row_inner][vc_column_inner][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

STEP 4: SHAKE IT OUT

[/vc_column_text][/vc_column_inner][/vc_row_inner][vc_row_inner][vc_column_inner][vc_single_image image="28492&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][/vc_column_inner][/vc_row_inner][vc_row_inner][vc_column_inner][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]Next you want to loosen the curls. “I like to use a large tooth comb or fingers to rake out the set and break the hair up giving a relaxed finish.” Says Brad.[/vc_column_text][/vc_column_inner][/vc_row_inner][vc_row_inner][vc_column_inner][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]
