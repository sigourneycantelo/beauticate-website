*We’re all guilty of it. Whether it’s lounging poolside for hours on end or surf-soaked summers that leave everything north of the shoulders looking and feeling a little drab. The Rastafarian results of hair neglect will leave you clamoring for your tangle teaser for the better part of an hour or worse, the scissors! So, in a bid to keep you in the good (and not-so-brassy books) with your colourist take note of these insider tips and tricks that’ll help you avoid the panic-chop. From the supplements supreme, crèmes du jour to the makeshift masks you can whip up in minutes the beauty magnates, bloggers and super-mod squad rescue remedies reveal all. Before all hope is lost, hair us out.*

1. "What's the essential hair product that will revitalize a model's thirsty locks in 30 seconds flat, even at the end of Fashion Week? Split-end repair treatments packed with hydrating agents, which bring new life to dull hair without weighing it down." – Delphine Courteille, Hairstylist and Designer at Studio 34. For a serum that’s split-end serious, try L’Oreal Absolut Repair Cellular Serum. The light and travel-friendly 50mL spray is ideal for those mid-summer weekends away or if you’re bouncing from beach to bar. Pack light ladies.

2. “Sometimes I put coconut oil in my hair, just on the ends overnight. That’s a really good treatment. I once used olive oil, but coconut oil smells much better.” – Miranda Kerr, Supermodel and founder of KORA Cosmetics.

An organic makeshift hair masque is an easy and cost efficient alternative to a salon applied treatment. Be sure to use organic coconut oil like [Indah Organics Hand Pressed Coconut Oil](http://indah.com.au/collections/coconut-oil/products/virgin-coconut-oil) and a couple of tablespoons of organic honey. Generously apply to damp hair, comb in from roots to ends, sectioning your hair as you apply. Rinse out after 40 minutes or so. Mother Nature’s completely sin-free answer to the conventional hair masque.

5. “Either way, I can tell you from my own personal experience over the years that both milk and bananas make my hair silky, smooth, and extra shiny.” – Crunchy Betty, Homemade Beauty Blogger. Now, the fact you can actually drink the leftovers should be enough of a sell, but if you need a little more convincing to smear mashed up bananas and dunk your hair in milk, consider the pros. Bananas are packed full of nutrients (namely folate and potassium) and have long sat at the top of the super-food chain before we’d even heard of holy kale. Whether you choose to use the over-ripe variety for a banana cake or beautification, this delicious recipe is well worth a read!
