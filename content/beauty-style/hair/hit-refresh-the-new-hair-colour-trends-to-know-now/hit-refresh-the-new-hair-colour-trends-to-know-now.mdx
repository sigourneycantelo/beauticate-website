faqs:
  - question: "What is a colourmelt hair technique?"
    answer: "A colourmelt is an evolution of the tortoiseshell trend that creates a subtle shade lift without harsh lines. It involves applying a base colour one shade lighter than your natural hair, then using balayage or freehand painting in complementary shades through the ends, working with your natural colour rather than against it."
  - question: "Is colourmelt suitable for brunettes?"
    answer: "Yes, colourmelt is particularly popular with brunettes who want to lighten up slightly without a dramatic change. The technique is designed to enhance natural brunette tones whilst creating dimension, making it a low-maintenance option that doesn't create noticeable regrowth lines."
  - question: "How do I maintain colourmelt hair colour?"
    answer: "To keep your colourmelt looking fresh, use colour-safe products like Oribe Bright Blonde Shampoo and Conditioner to prevent brassiness and maintain the tone. Since the technique works with your natural colour, it requires less frequent touch-ups than traditional full-colour treatments."
  - question: "What's the difference between colourmelt and ombré?"
    answer: "While ombré creates a more obvious gradient from dark roots to light ends, colourmelt uses subtle, blended transitions with complementary shades for a softer, more natural-looking result. Colourmelt is less maintenance-heavy and works harmoniously with your natural base colour rather than contrasting against it."

It’s fair to say many of us have hit a colour stalemate of late: our trusty friend, ombré has seen many iterations. From shombré to reverse ombré, it’s no surprise we’ve exhausted the style to mediocrity. Sure, it’s low maintenance and suits most of us; but 2016 is time to hit refresh on the trend guide for tinted locks. To help let go of tired old faithfuls, we’ve compiled the new hair-colour trends to get excited about and consulted hair colourist extraordinaire, Sheree Knobel, who gave the lowdown on the colour trends and hairstyle hints you’ll covet this year, and how to ask for them.

The trend gives hair a lived-in feel, not a freshly coloured vibe where the foils are straight from the scalp. Ask your colourist to work foils right up to the underneath areas along the part and then a dark blonde tone applied to the root area only, giving hair a slight shadow. Keep your hairline and ends nice and bright. Maintenance is every six to eight weeks, but can stretch out longer without needing to buy a hat.”
