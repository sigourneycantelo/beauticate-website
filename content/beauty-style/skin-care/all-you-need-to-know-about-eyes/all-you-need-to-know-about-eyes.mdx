## The Low-Down On Eyes

You might wonder why it is that we need to care for our eye area differently to the rest of our skin. Simply put, we put our eyes to work – a lot. Education Director at La Prairie, Belinda Besant, shares, “The skin around the eyes is thinner and more delicate than the skin on other parts of the body. This coupled with the perpetual motion these 22 muscles experience daily –  we blink around 10,000 times a day – makes the eyes the first place to show signs of ageing.”

Frowning, squinting, smiling, rolling your eyes in exasperation – we also rely on our eyes to convey our true thoughts and feelings to those around us. Add to that all the reading, watching, and scrolling we put our peepers through, and you can understand why the eye area needs some special attention. The skin around our eyes has fewer oil glands, which can lead to dryness and premature ageing (think wrinkles, loss of elasticity and sagging). And as we age, our collagen and elastin store drop, so extra care is needed.[/vc_column_text][vc_single_image image="39793&#8243; img_size="full"][vc_column_text]

## Massage Magic

You already know the benefits of massaging your face, so it should come as little surprise that incorporating a massage routine into your eye care ritual can work wonders, too. Our method of choice? Gently tap around the eye from up along the underside of the brow, and back down the cheekbone and under the inner eye, and repeat a few times. This will drain the excess fluid, and freshen and awaken your eyes.[/vc_column_text][/vc_column][/vc_row][vc_row][vc_column][vc_column_text]Be especially gentle while massaging the eye area as it's more delicate than other parts of the face. Belinda suggests using your ring finger when massaging and patting product onto eyes, then using a light gliding motion to smooth product in. A jade roller or gua sha, lightly applied, is also a good pick.[/vc_column_text][/vc_column][/vc_row][vc_row][vc_column][vc_column_text]

## Our Gaze Guide

As for the rest of the complexion, a focus on wellbeing is fundamental to keeping your eyes and the surrounding area healthy and beautiful. Belinda states, “As your eyes reflect what’s happening on the inside, getting more sleep, exercising and trying to follow a healthy diet will improve how your eyes look.” Staying hydrated is vital to keeping things looking plump and wrinkle-free, so aim to get your two to three litres of water every day.

A well-balanced diet is obviously also key. Consider upping your intake of salmon, as its abundance of omega 3s help nourish the delicate eye area skin (as well as helping improve eyesight). Other foods rich in good fats – nuts, seeds and avocados – are also beautifying (and delicious) additions to your diet. Carrots are famously beneficial to eye health – they’re rich in beta-carotene, which the body transforms into vitamin A, an essential component to good eyesight. Supplement these lifestyle changes with adding Skin Caviar Eye Lift to your morning and evening skincare routine, and you’ve got yourself a recipe for healthy, youthful eyes[/vc_column_text][vc_single_image image="39795&#8243; img_size="full"][vc_column_text]
