If you’ve missed the dermarolling and stamping movement that’s taken the skincare world by storm, then where on earth have you been? The idea of rolling or stamping your skin with tiny needles to create rejuvenating micro trauma and help products better penetrate has become de rigueur in dermal circles. These tools come with their own set of caveats, however. The main concern being that, in the uneducated hand, the needles can cause much more damage than intended. According to many leading dermatologists, dermarollers can drag the skin and create uneven punctures. There’s also a risk of infection if they’re not done in sterile-enough conditions.

## What is micro-infusion?

Micro-infusion is a form of at-home microneedling with a serum chamber built into the device. Instead of rolling, you stamp a head of tiny hollow needles across clean skin, and serum is delivered through the micro-channels as you go.

## Micro-infusion vs microneedling: what’s the difference?

So how is this any different to microneedling? Same idea, softer touch. Traditional microneedling and dermarolling drag tiny needles across the skin to create the micro-injuries that wake up collagen. Micro-infusion swaps the roller for a stamp. That means a controlled, even depth, and far less risk of tearing the skin.

Australian dermatologist <a href="https://drdavinlim.com/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Dr. Davin Lim</a> puts it beautifully. A little stamping device, its hollow needles finer than a single strand of hair, presses tailored serums into the upper layers of the skin. At an at-home depth of 0.5mm, the magic is in the delivery. You are flooding fresh micro-channels with active ingredients, and that is exactly why the glow arrives so fast. So think of at-home micro-infusion as your glow-and-delivery treatment. A beautiful weekly ritual, not a replacement for the real thing.

When you’re finished simply pat any leftover serum into the skin – all the way down to your neck. The result? One tip so it actually works. The most common complaint with this device is the serum not releasing. Fill the chamber with one full ampoule, then leave the device upside down for two to three minutes so the serum travels into the needles before you start. Don’t rush it.
