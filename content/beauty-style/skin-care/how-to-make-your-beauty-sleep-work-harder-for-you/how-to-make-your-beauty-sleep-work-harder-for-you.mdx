faqs:
  - question: "What should you put on your face before bed for glowing skin?"
    answer: "A nighttime routine should include a gentle cleanser, targeted treatment serum (like those with glycolic or lactic acid for resurfacing), and a nourishing moisturiser or sleeping mask. The best products work while you sleep to exfoliate, hydrate, and repair your skin overnight, so you wake with a refreshed complexion. Look for ingredients like squalane, retinol, and AHAs depending on your skin concerns."
  - question: "Is it better to use a night serum or sleeping mask?"
    answer: "Both serve different purposes and can be used together. Night serums are lightweight treatments that penetrate quickly to target specific concerns like dullness or fine lines, whilst sleeping masks are occlusive layers that lock in hydration and allow active ingredients to percolate throughout the night. Many beauty routines benefit from layering a serum first, then finishing with a mask for maximum efficacy."
  - question: "Can you use retinol and tanning products together at night?"
    answer: "Yes, but it requires the right formulation. Some advanced night products combine both retinol for anti-ageing and DHA for gradual tanning in a single mask, so you don't have to choose between treatments. This approach allows you to address multiple skin concerns simultaneously whilst you sleep, though always patch test first if you have sensitive skin."
  - question: "What's the best nighttime product for problem skin and blemishes?"
    answer: "Targeted spot treatments and lightweight sleeping masks designed for stressed or problematic skin are most effective for overnight blemish management. Look for products with calming ingredients like cica or those specifically formulated to reduce inflammation and clear breakouts whilst you sleep, such as overnight pimple patches or treatment serums that work without over-drying."
