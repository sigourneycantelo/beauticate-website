  - question: "What is IPL skin rejuvenation and how does it work?"
    answer: "IPL (Intense Pulsed Light) rejuvenation uses light energy to stimulate collagen production and improve skin texture and tone. It works by delivering fast bursts of heat and light to the skin's surface, with results that improve over weeks rather than immediately. There's no downtime, making it ideal before events or social occasions."
  - question: "How long does an IPL skin rejuvenation treatment take?"
    answer: "A typical IPL rejuvenation session takes around 10 minutes, making it a quick lunchtime or between-schedule appointment. The actual treatment time is brief, though you should allow extra time for a skin consultation and post-treatment advice from your therapist."
  - question: "What's the difference between IPL rejuvenation and laser pigmentation removal?"
    answer: "IPL rejuvenation focuses on overall skin texture, tone and radiance by stimulating collagen production, while laser pigmentation removal specifically targets dark spots and hormonal pigmentation. Clear Skincare Clinics recommends combining lasers with lightening creams for stubborn pigmentation issues rather than using IPL alone for this concern."

Now, don't get me wrong, I don't want to look Facetuned within an inch of my life, but I would like to look a little fresher. I'd like my skin to be high-beaming minus the need for a high rotation of highlighters.

For my treatment, I head to the Mosman clinic. When I front up I have a chat with Katerina, my lovely therapist, who I remember seeing several years ago. I decide to go for IPL rejuvenation as I have a fragrance launch party to go to this evening so can’t risk any flakiness. I’m told that it’s great to give the skin an immediate boost, which only improves as the weeks go on and will be perfect for my jampacked social schedule as there’s no downtime. I was surprised to discover that we wouldn’t be zapping my pigmentation today with the IPL machine – which is how I’ve experienced it in the past. Katerina explains that [Clearskincare Clinics](http://clearskincareclinics.com.au/) would instead devise a treatment plan of lasers combined with lightening creams to tackle the hormonal pigmentation I suffer from (note to self to book in for that). Using IPL for rejuvenation is a little more subtle but should hopefully leave me looking like I’ve been eating greens for a week.

Katarina removes my makeup and applies a cool gloopy gel over my face then holds the handpiece over segments of my face, there are fast bursts of heat and light which aren’t as painful as they used to be when they zapped the pigment, but still give me a little start anyway. It’s over in ten minutes, which is record time, and Katarina takes off the gel and sends me off with a Copper Peptide Serum to use tomorrow along with strict instructions to stay out of the sun.

Back at home I examine my skin. There’s no doubt about it. I’m noticeably glowy. The girls in the office say that my skin looks plumper – a bit like how your eyebrows look after they’ve been plucked – all pink and juiced-up. The effects will only get better over the next couple of weeks as the collagen has been heated and will continue to regenerate. Eureka! I’m off to throw my little Bec and Bridge dress now and for once I think I might skip the foundation. After all – we all want to see more natural, beautiful-looking skin in our social media feeds (and there’s not a lot a good concealer can’t deal with). Now bring on that party.
