The language of beauty is changing. For decades, we organised our entire skincare conversation around a single premise: you are ageing, and ageing is bad, and here is a product to slow it down. The word "anti-ageing" was shorthand for an entire industry.

That premise is being dismantled. Not for feel-good reasons, but for scientific ones.

The researchers who are doing the most interesting work on ageing right now aren't asking how to hide it. They're asking what actually causes it at a cellular level — and whether those mechanisms can be slowed, or even partially reversed. The answers are producing a new category that I'm calling longevity beauty. And unlike most beauty trend moments, this one has Nobel Prize-winning science underneath it.

## What's actually happening when we age

Visible ageing — wrinkles, loss of density, uneven tone, reduced radiance — is a symptom. The cause is happening inside cells, years before the surface changes appear.

Several interconnected mechanisms drive cellular ageing:

**NAD+ decline.** NAD+ (nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide) is a coenzyme present in every cell that powers energy production and DNA repair. Between age 20 and 50, NAD+ levels drop by approximately 50%. This decline impairs mitochondrial function (your cells' energy production), slows DNA repair, reduces collagen synthesis, and compromises the cellular processes that keep skin looking alive.

**Accumulated cellular damage.** Over time, cells accumulate damage faster than they can repair it. When damaged cells stop dividing but refuse to die, they become "senescent" — zombie cells that release inflammatory signals that damage surrounding tissue. The skin inflammation that characterises ageing skin is partly driven by senescent cell accumulation.

**Shortened telomeres.** Telomeres are protective caps on chromosomes that shorten with each cell division. When they become critically short, cells can no longer divide. Lifestyle factors — stress, poor sleep, smoking, ultra-processed foods — accelerate telomere shortening.

**Mitochondrial dysfunction.** As mitochondria age and accumulate damage, they produce less energy and more damaging reactive oxygen species. This creates a feedback loop: less energy means slower repair, slower repair means more damage.

Understanding these mechanisms changes what "effective skincare" even means.

## Why NAD+ precursors are the supplement of the moment

The most talked-about intervention in longevity circles right now is NAD+ precursor supplementation — specifically NMN (nicotinamide mononucleotide) and NR (nicotinamide riboside). You can't take NAD+ directly — it can't be absorbed through the gut — but these precursors convert to NAD+ efficiently once inside cells.

The research, much of it pioneered by Professor David Sinclair at Harvard, suggests that restoring NAD+ levels produces meaningful improvements in cellular function. Human trials have shown benefits in energy, muscle function, metabolic markers, and cognitive function. The skin-specific research is earlier-stage but mechanistically compelling: better cellular energy means better collagen synthesis, faster cell turnover, and improved DNA repair.

I've been taking 500mg of NMN daily for six months, for full transparency. I can't tell you my NAD+ levels have changed — I don't have a baseline measurement. What I can tell you is that my skin has a quality of aliveness that I notice in photos, and that my energy and recovery have improved in ways that are probably connected.

The challenge is quality. NAD+ precursor supplements vary enormously in purity and bioavailability. Look for third-party tested products with stable forms of NMN or NR.

## The longevity beauty stack

This isn't a supplement story alone. The practices that most powerfully support cellular longevity:

**Resistance training.** This is the intervention with the most evidence. Lifting weights stimulates NAD+ production, activates cellular repair pathways, increases collagen synthesis, and improves skin thickness and elasticity. I know it's not what anyone wants to hear. It is the most powerful anti-ageing intervention available. Twice a week is where meaningful change begins.

**Time-restricted eating.** Eating within an 8-10 hour window activates autophagy — your cells' cleaning process, which removes damaged proteins and cellular debris. This is the mechanism behind why fasting has shown anti-ageing effects in research. You don't need to fast dramatically — a 12-14 hour overnight window produces measurable benefits.

**Cold exposure.** Cold showers, cold plunges, or even ending your shower on cold activates cellular stress responses that are hormetic — beneficial stressors that make cells more resilient. The Wim Hof-adjacent wellness crowd isn't entirely wrong.

**Sleep architecture.** It's not just sleep duration — it's sleep quality. Deep sleep is when growth hormone peaks, cortisol clears, and cellular repair is most active. The practices that improve sleep quality (consistent sleep timing, dark and cool rooms, no screens before bed, magnesium supplementation) are also longevity practices.

## What to look for in longevity-focused skincare

The skincare industry is moving fast to meet this moment, with varying degrees of scientific legitimacy. The ingredients with the strongest cellular-level evidence:

**Retinoids.** The most studied topical ingredient for genuine structural change — increases collagen synthesis, accelerates cell turnover, repairs UV-damaged DNA. The cellular mechanism is well established. Non-negotiable in a longevity skincare routine.

**Antioxidants.** Free radical damage is a primary driver of cellular ageing. Topical antioxidants — vitamin C, vitamin E, ferulic acid, resveratrol — intercept oxidative damage before it accumulates. The Rationale Serum #1 is the antioxidant formulation I trust most in this category.

**Peptides.** Signal peptides communicate with fibroblasts to increase collagen and elastin production. Matrikine peptides (fragments of collagen that signal the need for repair) have the most convincing mechanism. Look for palmitoyl tripeptide-1 and palmitoyl tetrapeptide-7 at effective concentrations.

**Niacinamide.** Niacinamide is a form of vitamin B3 that directly supports NAD+ synthesis in skin cells, making it the rare topical ingredient with genuine cellular energy relevance. At 5-10%, it also repairs barrier, reduces inflammation, and improves skin tone.

## The honest truth about longevity beauty

The science is real. The timeline is long. You will not notice NAD+ supplementation in a week, or even a month. The cellular changes that longevity practices produce are structural and cumulative — they show up over years, not weeks.

But that's also what makes this different from the trend cycle I've watched spin for twelve years. Longevity beauty isn't a product moment. It's a practice. The people who will look genuinely different at 60 than their chronological age suggests are starting these practices now — in their 30s and 40s — not waiting until the visible ageing accelerates.

I find that more motivating than any before-and-after.
