## ANTIOXIDANT RAINBOW SALAD WITH FETTA & DATES

[/vc_column_text][/vc_column_inner][/vc_row_inner][vc_row_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

## INGREDIENTS:

¼ head of red cabbage

1 fennel bulb

½ lemon

1 large bunch of flat-leaf parsely

3 oranges

10 dates (harder, drier dates work best here)

10 stalks of thyme (it’s worth getting the fresh stuff over dried)

[/vc_column_text][/vc_column_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]150g of good quality fetta cheese

2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil

1 tsp honey

1 large garlic clove

Salt + Pepper

2 tbsp sesame seeds

A handful of pumpkin or sunflower seeds, toasted[/vc_column_text][/vc_column_inner][/vc_row_inner][vc_row_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

## METHOD

### Outsource the chopping

I recommend outsourcing the fine slicing to a food processor’s blade. You can also use a large knife. Start with the fennel bulb– the red cabbage will make everything it touches the colour of Barney the Dinosaur. Squeeze half a lemon over the fennel immediately to stop browning and set aside. Follow with a quarter of red cabbage.

[/vc_column_text][/vc_column_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_single_image image="27041&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][/vc_column_inner][/vc_row_inner][vc_row_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_single_image image="27043&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][/vc_column_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

### Manage your herbs

If you’re in a hurry, slice the leafy end of your bunch of parsley from the stalks and roughly chop. If you’re feeling fancy, pick the leaves from the stalks. Take each thyme stalk and draw your fingers backward along the stalk to remove the leaves. Roughly chop the thyme.

[/vc_column_text][/vc_column_inner][/vc_row_inner][vc_row_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

### Orange is the new orange

On a large chopping board, top and tail the 3 oranges to create a flat base. Using a large, sharp knife, pare away the rind in a downward motion, removing as much of the white pith from each orange as you can. Quarter and thinly slice the flesh, setting aside any orange juices that have run onto the chopping board.

[/vc_column_text][/vc_column_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_single_image image="27045&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][/vc_column_inner][/vc_row_inner][vc_row_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_single_image image="27046&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][/vc_column_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

### Date night

Quarter the dates and slice thinly. You want little tiny jewels of sweetness that will mix throughout the entire salad.

[/vc_column_text][/vc_column_inner][/vc_row_inner][vc_row_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

### Make the dressing

Crush your garlic clove and mix with 2 tbsp of olive oil, 1 tsp honey, and the excess orange juices, season generously with sea salt and cracked black pepper.

[/vc_column_text][/vc_column_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_single_image image="27047&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][/vc_column_inner][/vc_row_inner][vc_row_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_single_image image="27048&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][/vc_column_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

### My slaw of many colours

Now the fun part: Gently combine the fennel, red cabbage, sliced oranges, parsley, thyme, dates and sesame seeds with your dressing. Crumble the feta on top and scatter with toasted pumpkin seeds. A sprig or two of thyme anoints this salad perfectly.
