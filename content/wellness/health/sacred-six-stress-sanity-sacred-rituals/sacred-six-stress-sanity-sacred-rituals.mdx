Over the past year, I've been quietly building a whole new chapter of Beauticate. It's been exciting, fulfilling, creative and, if I'm honest, incredibly stressful at times.

In this solo Sacred Six episode, I'm sharing the six rituals, tools and practices that have helped me stay grounded, regulate my nervous system and protect my wellbeing during one of the busiest periods of my life.

From infrared saunas and nervous system support to community walks, breathwork and the surprising gadget that's become a daily non-negotiable, these are the habits helping me navigate long workdays, chronic pain, anxiety and overwhelm.

If you've been feeling stretched too thin, struggling with stress, burnout, poor sleep or simply trying to hold everything together, I hope this offers some practical inspiration — and a reminder that small rituals can have a profound impact.

## 1. Infrared sauna sessions

This has become my single most impactful tool for stress recovery. I'm using a Nook infrared sauna (code BEAUTICATE for a discount) three to four times a week, and the difference to my sleep quality and pain levels has been significant.

Unlike traditional saunas, infrared penetrates deeper into tissue, triggering an anti-inflammatory response and a post-session cortisol drop that carries into sleep. If you've been dismissing this as a luxury wellness trend, I'd encourage you to look at the research. It's not.

## 2. Community walks

This one surprised me most. There's something about walking alongside other people — not talking about work, not being productive, just moving through the world together — that does something no solo workout can replicate.

The Beauticate Walk & Talk WhatsApp group has become one of the most meaningful things we've built. If you're not already in it, join us. It costs nothing and the community is extraordinary.

## 3. Breathwork

I've been doing Brooke Stephenson's 9D breathwork sessions through the Luxe Wellness Collective and they have genuinely shifted something in me. Not in a vague wellness-speak way — I mean my baseline anxiety level is measurably lower in the days following a session.

If you've never tried structured breathwork (not just deep breathing — specifically 9D or holotropic styles), I'd start there before spending money on anything else on this list.

## 4. Meditation and sleep hygiene

I've used Insight Timer for years and it remains my recommendation for anyone struggling with sleep or anxiety. The free library is enormous. I use it nightly — a 20-minute guided sleep meditation has replaced the scrolling habit I'd let creep back in.

The habit stack that works for me: no phone after 9pm, magnesium glycinate, guided meditation, mouth tape (yes, I wrote about this — it works). Sleep is the foundation everything else sits on.

## 5. Neck and back tension relief

Chronic stress lives in the body, particularly the neck and upper back. I've been using the Kiicity neck massager daily (code BEAUTICATE for 20% off) and it's become as automatic as brushing my teeth. Ten minutes of heat and compression after a day at the desk resets the physical tension before it becomes pain.

A hot shower or bath aimed at the back of the neck and shoulders works similarly. The vagal nerve runs through that area — warmth there has a direct calming effect on the nervous system.
