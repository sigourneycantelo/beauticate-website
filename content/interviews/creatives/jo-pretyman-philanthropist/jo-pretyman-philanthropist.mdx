### “I’m really excited to be working with Sigourney on a collaboration with I-Manifest and Beauticate called the Style School.

Sig and I have been friends for years, we met back in my beauty PR days. Recently we were talking about the work I am doing with the young women of I-Manifest, sending them for internships and work experience, often to luxury brands and high end creative environments. The need has arisen for us to develop a school to help prepare them for this and for life in general. Sigourney sparked as I said it as she had also been working on a similar concept, a modern day finishing school (I think we both said “June Dally Watkins!” at the same time!). It’s a concept we both firmly believe in, teaching young people both old and new world finishing skills – skincare, makeup, deportment, style, and modern concepts such as personal brand and social media presence, voice and presentation training. We are really excited to see this program come together, both for I-Manifest young women and the broader community. We believe it will make a powerful purpose driven campaign for the right brand. Personal grooming, beauty and fashion are the most empowering tools, if presented in the right way.[/vc_column_text][/vc_column_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_single_image image="24772&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][/vc_column_inner][/vc_row_inner][vc_row_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_single_image image="24773&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][/vc_column_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

### The biggest challenge with starting I-Manifest was figuring out everything for myself along the way…

[But] I realised there was a need for this on a wider scale and also I could see a new way for doing charity, to make it more relevant and modern for everyone involved. So I-Manifest was born. I had never worked with kids, education, had little experience in the not-for-profit sector, so it has been a huge and continual learning curve. I think also being flexible enough to roll with it’s natural evolution while ensuring we stay true to the core purpose and vision. The biggest reward has been seeing a beautiful community grow, watching some of our first ever kids leave school and move to Europe to pursue their creative adventures. I love where I-Manifest has ended up in this way.[/vc_column_text][/vc_column_inner][/vc_row_inner][vc_row_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

### I always say it and it’s still true today. Trust yourself.

Of course you must hear lots of advice from multiple sources but always come back to your heart and gut to make sure the decisions are true to your vision and what you want to do. Just because something is a good idea doesn’t mean you have to pursue it. It’s easy to lose focus with the possibilities. There are three predominant spiritual guidelines that guide my life. Eckhart Tolle, [*The Power of Now*](http://www.angusrobertson.com.au/books/the-power-of-now-eckhart-tolle/p/9780340733509) – it’s kind of that ‘doink doink’ moment every time you hear his words. [*The Four Agreements*](https://www.booktopia.com.au/the-four-agreements-ruiz-don-miguel/prod9781878424310.html)by Don Miguel Ruiz and the *Five Reiki Precepts* by Dr Usui (the founder of reiki) which I learnt when I studied Reiki in my early 20s.[/vc_column_text][/vc_column_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_single_image image="24774&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][/vc_column_inner][/vc_row_inner][vc_row_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_single_image image="24775&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][/vc_column_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

### Growing up my grandmother Mimi was a huge life and beauty inspiration to me. She was perfection.

She was quite a (glamorous) rebel for her time, leaving her country town of Scone as a single young woman to live in Elizabeth Bay and work selling couture at David Jones. Her home and wardrobe in Balmoral was immaculate, her taste impeccable. We used to go on girl dates to David Jones Elizabeth St. Now I’m actually really inspired by the original Supermodels. I follow most of them on instagram. Because I think back in the day for them and still today beauty was about health, wellness, fitness and business. They were and still are strong, beautiful, healthy and visionary business women.[/vc_column_text][/vc_column_inner][/vc_row_inner][vc_row_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

### I’ve been obsessed with the cosmetic floor in David Jones since I was about 5 years old…

… it always felt like I was teleported to some magical alternate universe. I’m obsessed with skincare. My first real job was at Ella Baché for five years and I was involved in product development, so I learnt first hand just how much everything really does begin with the skin. I love Ella Baché Crème douce Démaquillant cleanser every day and tone with Clarins Toning Lotion with Chamomile or Armani Crema Nera Mineral Soothing Lotion. I am sucker for a spritz, lately I have been using [Ella Baché Radiance C Anti Oxidant Defence Mist](https://www.ellabache.com.au/products/radiance-c-antioxidant-defence-mist). I then use a serum; at the moment I’m using the Kiehl’s Hydro-Plumping Re-Texturising Serum Concentrate but I also love Clarins Double Serum for an extra boost. Following this I moisturise with either Armani Prima Glow or Ella Baché Eternal Plus Replenishing Day Cream.[/vc_column_text][/vc_column_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_single_image image="24776&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][/vc_column_inner][/vc_row_inner][vc_row_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_single_image image="24777&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][/vc_column_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

### I’ve just discovered [Frank Body](https://www.frankbody.com/au/)so it’s all about that for me at the moment…

… in the exfoliation department (including their lip scrub and balm!). I keep [it] in the shower and I try to remember to do twice a week, as I know how important it is for skin renewal. For body, I just [get] a good slather of [Salus Calendula and Shea Butter Hydrating Body Cream](http://salusbody.com.au/hydrate/) or Clarins Moisturising Body Lotion, twice a day, every day! At night, I remove my make up either with [Philosophy Purity face wipes](http://www.mecca.com.au/philosophy/purity-made-simple-one-step-facial-cleansing-cloths/I-024242.html) if I’m really tired or Shu Uemura Cleansing Oil and a hot towel for a more luxurious cleanse. Then a quick spritz of rose water spray that I buy at the Farmacia in Ibiza followed by an eye cream. Depending how dry my skin is feeling it’s then either the Weleda Evening Primrose Age Revitalising Night Cream or Kiehl’s Midnight Recovery Concentrate. Twice a week I do a mask (left on over night) of the Clarins Blue Orchid Face Treatment Oil mixed with some Ella Baché Hydra Mask.[/vc_column_text][/vc_column_inner][/vc_row_inner][vc_row_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

### My makeup routine is VERY simple.

In summer I can usually get away with a light coat of Laura Mercier Tinted Moisturiser. Winter, sans tan requires a bit more coverage so I use an Armani Fluid Master Primerfollowed my Armani Luminous Silk Foundation, which I also use at night in summer. When it comes to foundation, there simply is no other! For brows it’s a quick slick with a YSL pencil and then Armani mascara. I love Tom Ford for bronzer. For lips at the moment I’m just loving wearing red – MAC Lady Danger – which is a phase I’m especially embracing now as a single girl, after a recent summer Spanish romance and realising the impracticality of a boyfriend and red lipstick![/vc_column_text][/vc_column_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_single_image image="24778&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][/vc_column_inner][/vc_row_inner][vc_row_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_single_image image="24779&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][/vc_column_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

### I love fragrance, I feel incomplete without it.

I have so many I love! I always start with Clarins Eau Dynamisante – it’s kind of like my primer for fragrance! I call it the ‘mood spray’, so fresh and energising. I especially love to spritz it all over at the beach on a hot summer’s day. In summer for the day I love colognes, for that you can’t go past Acqua di Parma. At night and in winter I love anything woody, oud or tobac. [Comme des Garcon Wonderwood, Byredo 1996](http://www.mecca.com.au/comme-des-garcons/wonderwood-edp/V-008350.html) or Oud Immortel and Chanel Sycomore are on constant rotation. I’ve also recently discovered the Etat Libre d’Orange collection from Paris which are divine.[/vc_column_text][/vc_column_inner][/vc_row_inner][vc_row_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

### Mental health is also really important, I can struggle with that…

… and diet, sleep and exercise plays such a huge part in maintaining a strong, healthy mind. I ensure I am home several nights of the week and weekends to eat well, have a good night’s sleep and wake up for exercise. I am lucky I naturally gravitate towards healthy foods – green juice, kale and bone broth are pretty much a daily intake for me and I don’t really eat meat unless I’m out. I love sashimi, veggies and salad. I find it hard to drink as much water as I should so I often incentivise myself by buying a big bottle of Evian for the day as for me that doesn’t even taste like water – those crisp, mineral flavours of the French Alps are so delicious!"[/vc_column_text][/vc_column_inner][vc_column_inner width="1/2&#8243;][vc_single_image image="24780&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][/vc_column_inner][/vc_row_inner][/vc_column][/vc_row][vc_row][vc_column][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]Story by Rikki Hodge-Smith; Photography by [Myles Pritchard](https://www.instagram.com/mylespritchard/); Hair & Makeup to [Sarina Zoe](https://www.beauticate.com/who/sarina-zoe-makeup-artist-and-yogi)[/vc_column_text][/vc_column][/vc_row]
