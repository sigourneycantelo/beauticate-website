### "I never thought I could make a living from being a DJ, it was meant to be a hobby…

I began to fall in love with house music and I started going to festivals and nightclubs and hearing the music in context. I understood it and on a trip to Ibiza one year I decided that it was something I wanted to take up as a hobby. About 7 or 8 years ago – I’m getting old in DJ time – my friend was a DJ in Sydney and knew I wanted to do it. She needed a new DJ partner so she just started teaching me how to mix. We started DJ’ing together, and then she went overseas and did her own thing. I just kept doing my own thing. It’s always amazing when the crowd is really loving your music, such a great feeling.

### I think I’m a minimalist.

My mum always taught me less is more, so I guess the apple doesn’t fall far from the tree. I definitely stick to my old faithfuls. A new product will rarely make the cut, I guess I’m not very adventurous with trying new things any more. My skin can be a bit tricky so I try to stay away from using too many different products.

![1](/content/interviews/creatives/alice-quiddington-dj/1-2.jpg)


### I’ve got very sensitive skin…

…so I use [Cetaphil](http://bit.ly/2oUnqLr), it’s the only thing I can really use on my skin. It doesn’t make me break out and I love that. I used to use all different types of moisturisers until I tried that and I finally figured it out. I’d get blotches on my skin around my cheeks. It can get really itchy and dry. I was trying different things like diet and I thought once it may have been alcohol. I was cutting a whole bunch of things out. My sister went to see a dermatologist about her skin and they said to only use [Cetaphil](http://bit.ly/2oUnqLr), so I just tried it and it cleared up straight away. I can also use [Sunday Riley Good Genes](http://www.mecca.com.au/sunday-riley/good-genes-all-in-one-lactic-acid-treatment/I-015823.html) and [La Mer The Moisturising Soft Lotion](http://www.mecca.com.au/la-mer/the-moisturising-soft-lotion/I-024981.html) and [The Renewal Oil](http://www.mecca.com.au/la-mer/the-renewal-oil/I-022619.html). I’m pretty simple with my actual beauty routine.

### I get a little more adventurous with makeup.

I love Armani Luminous Silk Foundation, it’s just so velvety. And then I use [Nars Laguna and Casino bronzers](http://www.mecca.com.au/nars/bronzing-powder/V-000778.html), I swap between the two depending on the time of year. At the moment, I’m quite dark so I’m on Casino. I also love Estee Lauder blush, that’s probably my favourite – it’s Pure Color Envy Peach Passion. I use Bobbi Brown Shimmer Brick, it’s a good highlighter.

### I’m obsessed with Benefit Goof Proof Brow Pencil…

…it’s my new favourite product. I’ve just been basically doing really simple makeup lately, but am getting my lashes done at [The Lash Room in Double Bay](http://www.lashroom.com.au/). They’re awesome, I just get a quarter of the lash line done. I don’t really even need to wear mascara anymore. I also get my eyebrows done, and that’s kind of my makeup done.

![2 1](/content/interviews/creatives/alice-quiddington-dj/2-1-1.jpg)


### I use Redken on my hair…

…I just love Blonde Idol Custom Tone Violet shampoo, my hairdresser makes that up for me. I go to Belinda Jeffrey’s at La Boutique, she does the colour. Sometimes when you say you want a balayage, they just go nuts and just paint on it.

.

### My favourite fragrance is [Santal 33 by Le Labo](http://www.mecca.com.au/le-labo/santal-33/V-011641.html)…

…it’s my go-to, I love it. I also love [The Byredo’s La Tulipe](http://www.mecca.com.au/byredo/la-tulipe-edp/V-008293.html) and Tom Ford Tobacco Vanille. I like those really earthy, kind of spicy scents.

![3](/content/interviews/creatives/alice-quiddington-dj/3-2.jpg)


### Staying away from alcohol and eating healthy really has an impact on how my skin looks.

Sleep is really important for me, too – you can tell in my eyes and under my eyes when I haven’t had enough sleep. I try to get eight hours sleep per night, which isn’t always easy for a DJ. My sleep routine can be a bit all over the place so I try to overcompensate when I’m not working. The nights I’m not working I try to switch my phone airplane mode by 8:30pm and turn off any TV/laptop/technology, just read a book and have an early night. I try not to look at social media or emails in the morning. I will start the day with exercise or meditation before I begin to switch on any technology or get into work mode.

### I wear a lot of black.

I think I wear pretty classic, simple pieces, but couple it with accessories or shoes that are a little more out there."Story by Rikki Hodge-Smith; Interview by Sigourney Cantelo; Photography by [Alice Mahran](https://www.instagram.com/alicemahran/?hl=en)

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What skincare does Alice Quiddington use for sensitive skin?**
A: Alice uses Cetaphil as her primary skincare product, which cleared up the blotches and dry, itchy patches she had been experiencing. She was pointed toward it by her sister's dermatologist and says it is the only thing that does not make her break out.

**Q: What foundation does Alice Quiddington use?**
A: Alice loves Armani Luminous Silk Foundation, describing it as velvety. She pairs it with Nars Laguna or Casino bronzer depending on the season, Estee Lauder Pure Color Envy blush, and Bobbi Brown Shimmer Brick as a highlighter.

**Q: What is Alice Quiddington's sleep and wellness routine?**
A: Alice aims for eight hours of sleep and puts her phone on aeroplane mode by 8:30pm on nights she is not working, avoiding social media and emails until after morning exercise or meditation. She says diet and avoiding alcohol also have a visible impact on her skin.
