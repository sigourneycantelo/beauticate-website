### "When it comes to beauty, I always strive for flawless skin and create the rest of my look from there, so that all together it feels natural and simple.

A Beautyblender is the perfect tool to create flawless makeup. Keeping my skin hydrated is also key, so I use Ole Henriksen's products, specifically the Truth Serum, Banana Bright Eye Creme and a rich moisturiser, C-Rush Brightening Gel Creme, morning and night. Another must-have are my ongoing facials at [Melanie Grant](http://www.melaniegrant.com/), that always leave my skin feeling clean, refreshed and glowing.[/vc_column_text][vc_single_image image="30033&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

### I constantly say to my clients that you can have an amazing outfit, but if the hair and makeup aren’t right, you haven’t got a complete look.

People can always tell when you try too hard, so I think women look the most beautiful when they let their natural beauty shine through. That being said, my number one beauty essential is a [Spray Aus spray tan](https://sprayaus.com.au/).[/vc_column_text][vc_single_image image="30034&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

### I have my morning routine pretty much down pat.

I cleanse with [Go-To Properly Clean](https://www.gotoskincare.com/products/face/properly-clean), then I’ll use a toner and [Go-To moisturiser](https://www.gotoskincare.com/products/face/very-useful-face-cream)before makeup. By Terry Lumi Serum is such a great base to apply before foundation – it gives the skin just the right amount of glow and acts as a natural colour correcting serum.

For my base, I try to use a product with SPF in it to avoid damage from the sun. Next, I use my [NARS Sheer Glow Foundation](https://www.mecca.com.au/nars/sheer-glow-foundation/V-006868.html) and liquid blush to give me a glowy complexion. The [Kevyn Aucoin The Volume Mascara](https://www.mecca.com.au/kevyn-aucoin/the-volume-mascara/I-002418.html) is a must-have as I love a big lash.[/vc_column_text][vc_single_image image="52879&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

### It’s fun to get a little more dramatic with my makeup for a night out.

I use the same [NARS foundation](https://www.mecca.com.au/nars/sheer-glow-foundation/V-006868.html) and build it up to get good coverage. I like this one as it gives me a soft matte finish, which pairs well with a smokey eye and smudged eyeliner. Tarte's eyeshadow palettes are great and Sephora’s gel eyeliners are so easy to use. [Ardell's individual lashes](http://bit.ly/2MwV9aU) are always a favourite of mine and then I go in with Becca’s Shimmering Skin Perfector Luminous Blush, which is the bomb for your cheeks. I ensure my eyebrows are thick and filled in as they emphasise my eyes, and [Amy Jean’s new eyebrow pencil](https://amyjean-collection.com/collections/shop-collection) is fab for this.[/vc_column_text][vc_single_image image="52887&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

### I have a couple of favourite makeup tricks that are easy to do but really work.

If you have straight eyelashes like I do, my first tip is to apply mascara before curling with an eyelash curler. I find applying product before curling maintains the curl all day. I also like to elongate my eyes, so I tend to apply bronzer or a bronze shadow to the outer corners of my eyes and blend it outwards.[/vc_column_text][vc_single_image image="52881&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

### Because I colour my hair so frequently, I know I need to take care of it.

To offset the damage of using hot tools, I like to use ELEVEN Miracle Hair Treatment and their Hydrate My Hair shampoo and conditioner. On days at home, I like to air dry my hair and keep the heat off it.

[/vc_column_text][vc_single_image image="30035&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

### I would describe my personal style as classic monochrome with injections of colour.

I like to mix it up and play with trends, but my style is fundamentally classic. I make sure I invest in timeless essentials that can be worn after trends fade. My favourite Australian designers are Toni Maticevski, Dion Lee, Acler and Ellery, plus international brands such as Christian Dior and Chanel. These designers have a timeless element, but also evolve and keep their pieces fresh and stylish. I’m all about strong details and hero pieces. I just bought the Balenciaga Knife Pumps, which add a fun edge to any outfit.

[/vc_column_text][vc_single_image image="52882&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

### I started my career pre-social media, so I never really knew it was something I could pursue.

I’ve always had a love of fashion and took pride in curating my own outfits, but it was only through my job in fashion marketing at Westfield that I saw potential for a styling career. I worked for many years as a Project Marketing Manager and then would work tirelessly after hours and on weekends styling, to build my portfolio. I knew I was passionate about styling and that really grew over time.

[/vc_column_text][vc_single_image image="30037&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

### Seeing the value that my job brings to small businesses and those just starting out gives me the passion to work as hard as I do.

Driving sales for brands and helping them to achieve their goals is so fulfilling. Another great part of my job is helping women to look and feel their best. I love when I know that a client feels amazing – it’s such a great process to be a part of.

[/vc_column_text][vc_single_image image="52888&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

### I think I’m better than I used to be at saying no when I’m exhausted. Previously I’d have a hard time with this, but as the years go on saying no is my own form of self care.

I’ve just started reformer Pilates at [Elite Training Sports and Medicine](http://elitetsm.com.au/). As a working mum, having a strong core is imperative and I try and do this twice a week at about 5.30am with Stephanie Dion so I don’t impact my family. This way I’ve also done something for myself before I even start my day. For so long I’ve struggled with weakness in the abs after two pregnancies, but I’m finally starting to feel stronger and have much less backpain.

[/vc_column_text][vc_single_image image="52884&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

### I think it’s important to not be too hard on yourself about what you eat.

I do try to be as healthy as possible, but I have a huge sweet tooth and when I really want chocolate, I think it's best just to have it and really enjoy it It’s harder for me to stick to a daily routine because everyday is so different for me, but morning coffee seems to be my one lasting ritual.

[/vc_column_text][vc_single_image image="52885&#8243; img_size="full" alignment="center" css_animation="fadeIn"][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeIn"]

### I really wish I had more time to relax, but to be honest my schedule is a bit crazy at the moment.

I have two young girls, so when I’m not working I love to spend time with them. That is when I’m at my most relaxed – other than during my alone time with my morning cup of coffee, which tends to be my own kind of meditation before the day begins. I always like to sit and get some work done by myself nice and early, because once the kids wake up the madness begins!"
