### My beauty and makeup philosophy is less is more…

… and the more natural you are, the prettier I think people come across. When I’m not wearing any makeup I like to just be moisturised, dewy and balmy. ### I will never fall asleep with makeup on. Like, ever…

… no matter what the circumstances are. I say that with the utmost certainty. I can never lie and say that good skin isn’t genetic, because it definitely is. I think it’s diet, genetics and the way you take care of it. Everybody’s skin is different, but I make sure I take really good care of mine.[/vc_column_text][/vc_column][/vc_row][vc_row css=".vc_custom_1528872736472{padding-bottom: 30px !important;}"][vc_column width="1/2&#8243;][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeInUp"]

### My mum would put almond oil on my skin as a kid…

… and it really taught me the benefits of oil. I am more of a fan of coconut oil and that's why it was so important for me to include it in the ModelCo range. – coconut oil is in most of the products. But, I especially love the Coconut Luxe Beauty Oil – you can literally use it for everything; hair, face, body, just everything. Mum was big on things being organic. She was into all natural everything in my house. ### I always make sure I don’t have makeup on before I get on a flight.

If I'm going from a job straight to the airport, I will keep makeup wipes in my bag. As well as a good moisturiser, just until I get to wherever I’m going to properly wash my face. I'll also take the Glow Beauty Balm just so I can have really light coverage and to not feel like I'm wearing makeup, but it makes my skin look really clear. And I drink a lot of water on flights. I feel sick on flights, probably because of the air pressure, so I have multi-vitamins with me.[/vc_column_text][/vc_column][/vc_row][vc_row css=".vc_custom_1528872736472{padding-bottom: 30px !important;}"][vc_column width="1/2&#8243;][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeInUp"]

### I've had to teach myself to just spend time on the couch.

To relax, I like to just go home. It’s so funny for me because when I am away from home for a long period of time I can’t wait to get home, but if I am home for too long I think, what can I do? I just go home and catch up on TV shows or watch movies I haven’t seen before. I literally live next door to my gym so I wake up every morning and the billboard for my gym is the first thing I see, so I might do that. My job isn’t very consistent – I am one place one week, I am here and there and travelling. When I am home I really enjoy getting into a routine.[/vc_column_text][/vc_column][vc_column width="1/2&#8243;][vc_single_image image="19253&#8243; img_size="full" css_animation="fadeInUp"][vc_custom_heading text="SHE'S LEARNING TO TAKE TIME OUT TO CHILL OUT" font_container="tag:h3|text_align:center" use_theme_fonts="yes" css_animation="fadeInUp" el_class="img-title"][/vc_column][/vc_row][vc_row css=".vc_custom_1528872736472{padding-bottom: 30px !important;}"][vc_column width="1/2&#8243;][vc_single_image image="19254&#8243; img_size="full" css_animation="fadeInUp"][vc_custom_heading text="SHE WAS A PRIMA BALLERINA IN ANOTHER LIFE" font_container="tag:h3|text_align:center" use_theme_fonts="yes" css_animation="fadeInUp" el_class="img-title"][/vc_column][vc_column width="1/2&#8243;][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeInUp"]

### I was a ballet dancer for 12 years…

… so I’m very good at treating injuries. I broke my ankle, I broke my foot twice, I fractured a vertebra and I snapped my hamstring. I still have things that hurt and crack in weird disgusting ways. People literally think I am breaking something when I move certain ways. I am still super flexible though and my muscle memory is insane. I now work out with a trainer and I do boxing and Pilates.[/vc_column_text][/vc_column][/vc_row][vc_row css=".vc_custom_1528872736472{padding-bottom: 30px !important;}"][vc_column width="1/2&#8243;][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeInUp"]

### I just turned 20, so I have the metabolism of a 20 year old and can get away with eating ‘cheaty’ foods.

I try not to do that, but I’ll eat a cheeseburger and mac and cheese. Pretty much everything with starch, gluten, dairy and sugar is what I consider cheating on eating well. But it’s also ok to eat anything that makes you happy, in moderation. I can cook actually. Two of my friends were just in the Victoria’s Secret show and they really wanted to eat something healthy but we didn’t want to go anywhere. So I went over and cooked them a nice meal before I sent my little kiddies off. I did a chicken teriyaki stir-fry, but I didn’t really use any salt. I also added in chopped veggies with spinach and carrots, then I did mashed sweet potatoes with no butter or salt.[/vc_column_text][/vc_column][vc_column width="1/2&#8243;][vc_single_image image="19257&#8243; img_size="full" css_animation="fadeInUp"][vc_custom_heading text="SHE CREDITS GOOD GENES, YOUTH AND RELATIVELY HEALTHY EATING HABITS FOR HER FIGURE." font_container="tag:h3|text_align:center" use_theme_fonts="yes" css_animation="fadeInUp" el_class="img-title"][/vc_column][/vc_row][vc_row css=".vc_custom_1528872736472{padding-bottom: 30px !important;}"][vc_column width="1/2&#8243;][vc_single_image image="19256&#8243; img_size="full" css_animation="fadeInUp"][vc_custom_heading text="EFFORTLESSLY CHIC" font_container="tag:h3|text_align:center" use_theme_fonts="yes" css_animation="fadeInUp" el_class="img-title"][/vc_column][vc_column width="1/2&#8243;][vc_column_text css_animation="fadeInUp"]
