Welcome to episode three of our Sacred Spaces series, proudly sponsored by [King Living](https://www.kingliving.com.au/). When I first dreamt up this idea of sacred spaces, one of the very first places that came to mind was [Soma](https://somabyron.com.au/), the wellness retreat in the Byron Bay hinterland founded by my own meditation teacher, [Gary Gorrow](https://garygorrow.com.au/).

I learnt Vedic Meditation from Gary thirteen years ago, when I was pregnant with Max. He is known for teaching big names and CEO types, yet he always remains so humble and gentle. He taught me some of the most profound lessons of my life, ones I still use today. So when I was in Byron recently, it felt perfect to sit down with him in his own sacred space and capture twenty years of his learnings in one conversation.

## Two Decades of Learnings in One Hour

This episode is exactly what it promises, two decades of Gary's wisdom distilled into a single hour. We talk about what twenty years of teaching has taught him about the mind, about anxiety, and about how a simple daily practice can change the whole shape of your life. Some of the techniques Gary describes I have been using myself, and they are genuine game changers.

There was a retreat on the day we filmed, so we caught up in Gary's gorgeous home, which sits on site next door to the main house and is very much designed in the spirit of Soma. It was a rainy day, but it felt so cosy inside, with his beautiful cat Leopold purring between us.

## Healing the Nervous System, Calming Anxiety

In this conversation we learn all about Gary's upbringing on Sydney's Northern Beaches and his struggle with depression and burnout while he was working in film and TV production. Gary found meditation, learnt how to heal his nervous system and shift his mindset, and from there he says he answered a calling to become a meditation teacher.

So much of what he shares comes back to anxiety, how to soften it, how to sit with it, and how a regular practice can return you to a calmer baseline. It is the kind of gentle, practical wisdom that comes only from years of doing the work himself.

## Meditation, Mindset and Maximising Success

Gary is known for teaching executives and high performers, and there is a reason so many of them keep coming back. He shares his thinking on how a steady meditation practice supports clarity, resilience and better decision making, and how inner peace and outer success are far from opposites.

We also talk about building a meaningful practice that lasts, rather than something you reach for only in a crisis, and how creating spaces that foster calm can hold the whole thing together.

## Soma, A Sacred Space for Healing

Gary built Soma with his brother, designer and Ksubi co-founder George Gorrow, and the result is a feat of architecture and design. As a sanctuary for healing and transformation, Soma pays respect to the sacred energy of the land it occupies, nine hectares of tropical countryside that includes a lotus pond and a tranquil bamboo garden, and features the iconic geodesic dome, the perfect place to practise yoga and meditation while feeling at one with nature.

Soma was also the set of the series Nine Perfect Strangers, filmed there during COVID. If you love this conversation, you can hear more about the design and philosophy of the retreat in our companion episode, [Gary Gorrow on Designing Sacred Spaces](/vodcast/episodes/vedic-meditation-teacher-gary-gorrow-on-designing-sacred-spaces).
