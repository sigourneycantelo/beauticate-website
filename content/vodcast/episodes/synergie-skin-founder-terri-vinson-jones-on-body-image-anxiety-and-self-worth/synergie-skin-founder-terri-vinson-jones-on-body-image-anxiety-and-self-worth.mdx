Terri Vinson Jones is a cosmetic chemist, a scientist and the founder of Synergie Skin, the brand adored by dermal therapists and beauty editors alike. Behind that powerhouse persona is a story of quiet resilience, deep trauma and an unwavering drive. This is one of the most honest conversations we've had on Beautiful Inside.

## Finding Out She Was Adopted

Terri was twelve when she overheard the truth that would reshape her whole sense of self.

> "I was told I was adopted, and I was twelve. You feel like you've just been plonked somewhere."

What moves me most is what she has done with that uncertainty. Rather than let it define her, she chose to own it.

> "I'm the sum total of all those impacts. And it's up to me how I deal with that."

## Growing Up Fast

Terri grew up with a mentally unwell mother, and the pressure to hold things together took root early. She is candid about the perfectionism that followed, and about her recovery from an eating disorder, which she shares not for sympathy but because she knows how many women carry the same story quietly.

![Terri as Miss Teenage Victoria in 1981](/content/vodcast/episodes/synergie-skin-founder-terri-vinson-jones-on-body-image-anxiety-and-self-worth/synergie-skin-founder-terri-vinson-jones-on-body-image-anxiety-and-self-worth-1.jpg "Terri as Miss Teenage Victoria, 1981.")

## A Woman in Science

Building Synergie Skin meant backing herself in rooms that didn't always make space for her. Terri talks about imposter syndrome in male-dominated science, and about learning to trust her own expertise. It's a quietly radical thing, a woman claiming authority in a world that kept asking her to prove it.

## Anxiety, Divorce and Asking for Help

Terri is refreshingly open about her mental health, and about the season when she needed extra support.

> "When I was going through my divorce, I took antidepressants, and it did help."

For someone in the wellness world to say that so plainly matters. She speaks about her anxiety, her tendency to overthink, and her curiosity about whether ADHD is part of her picture, all without shame.

## The Work That Changed Everything

What turned it around was the inner work. Therapy, deep self-awareness and a slow, hard-won self-compassion. Terri is living proof of the idea that runs through her whole story, that our deepest pain can shape our strongest purpose.

## Why It Stayed With Me

Terri's life is a quiet argument that beauty isn't skin deep, and that success doesn't mean perfection. I left this one thinking about how much we carry from childhood, and how much we get to decide what we do with it. I think you will too.
