For this episode the tables are turned and I am the one getting grilled. My friend [Georgie Abay](https://thegracetales.com/), an amazing journalist and the editor of Marie Claire, suggested I do an episode where I share the backstory of how this podcast came to be and the experiences that have shaped its purpose. I also figured that if I expect my guests to be completely vulnerable and bare all, it is only fair that I share my own story.

## Turning the tables

This interview is deeply personal for me. I open up about my childhood and the lead up to my decision to go to a treatment centre for depression and anxiety. Thankfully Georgie was there to support me through all of it.

## Sixteen years of friendship

Georgie and I first met more than 16 years ago at Vogue, where we quickly became close friends. We are godmothers to each other's children and have been through all the hard stuff together. Georgie is not only a fantastic journalist but also the former host of her own podcast and the founder of [The Grace Tales](https://thegracetales.com/), a media site dedicated to motherhood. I have always admired her creativity, determination and entrepreneurial spirit, and she constantly encourages me to step out of my comfort zone.

## Why I left Vogue for Beauticate

In this intimate conversation we reminisce about our magazine days, explore why I left Vogue to start Beauticate, and get into the pivotal, life-changing experiences that led to the creation of this podcast. It is a lot to cover, but I am hoping that by sharing my story others will feel validated, and even encouraged. So many of us suffer in a silent vortex of emotion, feeling that if we speak up or ask for help we will be labelled weak or problematic. These feelings are far more widespread and universal than we think, and opening up the dialogue around mental health can only help us feel more accepted and heard.

## Support and resources

I stayed at South Pacific Private Hospital in Curl Curl, NSW, Australia.

If you are interested in 12 step support groups for childhood trauma you can check out [Adult Children of Alcoholics and Dysfunctional Families](https://adultchildren.org/).

Or if you think you may be codependent and want to explore 12 step support groups for that, look into CODA.

If this has triggered you and you would like to investigate the help available to you, [Beyond Blue](https://www.beyondblue.org.au/) and [Lifeline](https://www.lifeline.org.au/) are great places to start in Australia.
