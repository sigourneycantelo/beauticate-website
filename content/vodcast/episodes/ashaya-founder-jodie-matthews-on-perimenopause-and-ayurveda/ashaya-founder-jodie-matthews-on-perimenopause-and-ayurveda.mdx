## Why Ayurveda

After years leading some of the biggest names in beauty, including MAC, Jurlique and O&M Haircare, Jodie devoted herself to studying naturopathy when her own body started to change. What she kept returning to was Ayurveda, the ancient Indian science of life that treats the body as a sacred home to protect and nurture. It is the philosophy that gives [ashaya](https://ashaya.com.au/) its name and its heart, and Jodie founded the brand with Siobhan Fildes and Sarah Ward to bring that wisdom to other women.

## Understanding Your Doshas

One of the most grounding ideas Jodie shares is that there is no single right way to feel well. In Ayurveda we each have a unique constitution made up of three energies, or doshas, Vata, Pitta and Kapha. When you understand which one is dominant for you, you can start to read your own imbalances and choose the foods, rituals and herbs that bring you back to centre. For Jodie, this was a revelation during perimenopause, when the old rules stopped working and her body needed a more personal approach.

## Tending Your Digestive Fire

If there is one practical shift to take from this episode, it is the Ayurvedic idea of Agni, your digestive fire. Jodie stopped fasting in the mornings to support her Agni, leaned into organic whole foods, and reduced alcohol to give her liver room to clear hormones gently. She also kept moving in ways that felt nourishing rather than punishing, with yoga, walking, paddleboarding and Pilates. None of it is about restriction. It is about working with your body instead of against it.

## Jodie's Daily Herbs

Out of her own experience came [ashaya](https://ashaya.com.au/), and the herbs she now takes daily. She reaches for Curcumin to support inflammation, Ashwagandha for libido and energy, and Saffron for mood, all of which she has woven into the [ashaya Re-Glow Perimenopause Relief](https://ashaya.com.au/products/re-glow-perimenopause-relief). She is honest that there are still days she struggles, four years in, but the natural approach has given her a sense of being more prepared, connected and positive about this season of life.
