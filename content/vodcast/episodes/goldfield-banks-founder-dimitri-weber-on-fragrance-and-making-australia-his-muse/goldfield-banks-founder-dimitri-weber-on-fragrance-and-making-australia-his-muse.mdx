I met Dimitri Weber in his home in Port Douglas, deep in the tropics, after a few magical days together learning about Goldfield & Banks and the wood at the heart of his fragrances. You probably know the brand as the luxury Australian scent house making waves around the world. What you might not know is the deeply personal story that built it.

This is a heartfelt, intimate conversation. We talk about trauma and therapy, perfectionism and heartbreak, and why scent became Dimitri's most powerful storytelling tool.

## A Childhood on the Water

Dimitri grew up somewhere most of us can't imagine. His parents ran a shipping business in Europe, so for his earliest years home was a barge, drifting the canals and rivers of France.

> "I lived until I was five and a half, until I went to boarding school."

Being sent away so young left its mark.

> "It was very tough. I was crying for the first few years, missing my mum, missing my dad, missing my home."

What stayed with him was scent. He describes the smell of the old stone locks as the boat passed through them, and the word he reaches for is the one that runs through his whole collection. Serenity.

## The Years in Fragrance

A passion for perfume showed up early, and it never left. Dimitri worked the department-store floors of Belgium and Paris, then spent years with the great French houses through the YSL and Gucci era. He loved it. He also never quite switched off.

> "I've always been a perfectionist."

> "I think I became a workaholic. I had no choice."

## The Heartbreak That Changed Everything

After fourteen years, a relationship ended, and with it the comfortable life he had built. He is honest about how hard it was to be suddenly alone, and about the fear of abandonment that traced all the way back to that boarding-school boy.

> "It was quite traumatic to come from this very comfortable life, and all of a sudden you're on your own."

Therapy helped. So did learning, slowly, that solitude didn't have to mean loss.

> "I'm very happy to be on my own now. I love being on my own."

## The Moment Australia Had No Perfume

Work brought Dimitri to Australia, and a quiet moment in a Sydney department store changed the course of his life. He asked a beauty advisor for a local luxury fragrance.

> "She said, there's nothing in the market. We don't have anything. And I thought, that's very strange."

He couldn't let it go.

> "This country has an abundance of essential oils, an abundance of botanicals that can be used in fragrances. But no one had really had the idea of doing something with it."

## Building Goldfield & Banks

So he did. Founded in 2016, Goldfield & Banks captures Australia from desert to rainforest, built on native botanicals like boronia, sandalwood and finger lime, ingredients Dimitri treats as touchstones of memory and emotion. The local market was slow to back him, so he proved it overseas first, then watched the buyers come home to him. The moment it landed is one he still keeps a photo of.

> "I thought, oh my God, I've made it. I made it. It was that moment more than anything else."

## On Stillness and Solitude

For a self-confessed workaholic, Dimitri has built a life with real calm in it. Morning swims, big walks, eight hours of sleep, yoga and good food cooked by his partner Ricky. He has made peace with being alone, and found a kind of strength in it.

> "Nothing scares me anymore."

There's a French word he uses for what a fragrance leaves behind once you've walked past. Sillage.

> "It's what you leave behind. It's the trail."

It's a fitting idea for a man whose whole life has been about scent, memory and the quiet mark we make on the world.

## Why It Stayed With Me

Dimitri is proof that the things that break us can become the things that build us. A barge, a boarding school, a heartbreak, a country far from everything he knew. He turned all of it into beauty. I left Port Douglas with even more respect for what goes into a bottle, and for the man who taught Australia to smell itself.
