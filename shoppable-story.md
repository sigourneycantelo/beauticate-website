# Shoppable stories

> How to build a single-brand or product-led story that converts. For Claude and Claude Code when a piece is built around one brand or product. Read CLAUDE.md section 12 for the structure. This file is the steps. Worked example: `eir-women-conversion-layout.md`.

## When to use this

When a story centres on one brand or product: a founder piece, a paid partnership, or a tried-and-tested review. For ordinary editorial, use `docs/updating-stories.md` instead.

## What I need from you

- The draft, or the author's words to build around.
- Whether it's a founder piece, a paid partnership or independent editorial. This sets the disclosure.
- The products to feature, with prices and Shopify or affiliate links.
- The author, with bio and photo.
- Any exclusive reader code or lead magnet, if one exists.

If any are missing, ask. Never invent prices, links, claims or codes.

## Steps

1. Follow the 10-part structure in CLAUDE.md section 12.
2. Preserve the author's words. Add the conversion modules around them, never rewrite their voice. Mark anything you add.
3. Put a disclosure line up top only if there's a commercial relationship. Add an author box either way.
4. Place the first shop module at the turn, after the education, not before. Use the card from CLAUDE.md section 8, no button.
5. Keep product claims experiential, not medical or absolute. Especially for health, beauty and supplements.
6. Float pull quotes away from their source line.
7. Add the FAQ. Write lead answers so AI search can lift them.
8. Add the offer, and the email capture where one exists.
9. Add schema: Article, FAQPage and Product (CLAUDE.md section 13).
10. Save as a draft, share a preview and the checklist.

## Rules that always apply

- Structure: CLAUDE.md section 12. Cards: section 8. House style: section 5. Schema: section 13.
- Disclose up top when there's a commercial tie. Educate before selling. One repeated call to action.
- Never invent prices, links, quotes, statistics, codes or claims. Ask.
- Preserve the author's words.

## Definition of done

- [ ] Disclosure up top if commercial, author box always
- [ ] Education comes before the first shop module
- [ ] Cards correct, own vs affiliate, no button, footnote if mixed
- [ ] Claims experiential, not medical or absolute
- [ ] Pull quotes not next to their source
- [ ] FAQ with lead answers
- [ ] Offer present, email capture if one exists
- [ ] Schema: Article, FAQPage, Product
- [ ] House style applied
- [ ] Saved as a draft, preview shared

## If something's missing

Ask in one short list. Usual gaps: final prices, product links, the reader code, the author bio and photo, and whether it's a paid partnership.
