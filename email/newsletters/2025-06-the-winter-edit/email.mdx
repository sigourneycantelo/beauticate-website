Winter has a way of revealing the truth about your skincare routine.

The products that skated by in summer — fine textures, single-layered moisturisers, the occasional serum you used when you remembered — suddenly feel inadequate when the cold strips back your skin's defences.

This month, six products have earned permanent spots in our editors' cold-weather arsenals. One of them surprised us.

---

## What we're using

**The barrier repairer.** We've written about ceramides before, but this winter we're all in. Dr. Jart+ Cicapair Serum has been quietly sitting in our beauty closet for years, and this is the season it finally makes sense — redness from heating systems, tightness after cleansing, sensitivity that appears from nowhere. Ceramide formulas address all of it at the source.

**The overnight treatment.** Sunday Riley Luna Sleeping Night Oil has graduated from occasional use to every-night ritual. The blue tansy brings down inflammation overnight; the trans-retinoic acid ester is gentle enough that you wake up bright rather than peeling. Winter skin responds to it differently than summer skin — it drinks it in.

**The body treatment we forgot about.** Frank Body Creamy Face Scrub repurposed as a body product sounds indulgent. It is. Twice a week on dry winter legs, it changes everything.

---

## The ingredient conversation

We've been spending time with the research on polyglutamic acid — a humectant that holds more moisture than hyaluronic acid and sits on top of the skin barrier rather than penetrating it. The result is plumping without the tight, dehydrated follow-up that some people experience with HA.

Worth watching. We're testing three formulas currently and will report back in the next issue.
